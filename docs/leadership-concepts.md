## What is Leadership?

* Leadership is the ability to inspire, guide, and support a team or organization toward a shared vision while fostering trust, accountability, and growth.
* A leader earns respect through their actions, decision-making, and the way they treat others.
* A leader creates a positive, high-performing environment where people feel valued and driven to do their best.

## Strong Leader Characteristics

* **Vision & Purpose** – A strong leader provides a clear direction and motivates others toward a common goal.
* **Integrity & Ethics** – They lead with honesty, fairness, and strong moral principles.
* **Communication Skills** – They actively listen, provide clear instructions, and foster open dialogue.
* **Empathy & Emotional Intelligence** – They understand and respect the emotions and perspectives of their team.
* **Decisiveness & Problem-Solving** – They make informed, confident decisions and address challenges effectively.
* **Accountability** – They take responsibility for their actions and decisions while holding others accountable.
* **Inspiration & Motivation** – They encourage and uplift their team, recognizing and rewarding contributions.
* **Adaptability & Resilience** – They embrace change and stay calm under pressure.
* **Empowering Others** – They delegate, trust their team, and invest in their growth.
* **Leads by Example** – They embody the values and work ethic they expect from others.

## Employee Drift


1. Refers to a gradual decline in an employee's engagement, motivation, or alignment with company goals over time. It can manifest as reduced productivity, lack of enthusiasm, disengagement from team activities, or even a shift in priorities that lead employees to seek opportunities elsewhere.
2. **Causes**

   
   1. Lack of Career Growth

      
      1. Employees may feel stagnant if they don't see opportunities for advancement.
   2. Poor Management or Leadership

      
      1. Ineffective communication, lack of recognition, or micromanagement can push employees away.
   3. Misalignment with Company Vision

      
      1. If employees feel disconnected from the company's mission, they may become disengaged.
   4. Work-Life Imbalance

      
      1. Burnout from excessive workloads can lead to detachment.
   5. Cultural Fit Issues

      
      1. Employees may feel out of place if the work environment doesn't align with their values.
3. **Prevention**

   
   1. Regular Check-ins & Feedback

      
      1. Frequent conversations about career goals and job satisfaction.
   2. Growth & Development Opportunities

      
      1. Training, promotions, and skill-building initiatives.
   3. Recognition & Rewards

      
      1. Acknowledging employee contributions to keep morale high.
   4. Strong Company Culture

      
      1. Fostering a workplace where employees feel connected and valued.
   5. Work-Life Balance Support

      
      1. Assisting team member establish & maintain work / life balance.

## Feedback Sandwich


:::info
The **feedback sandwich** is a method of delivering constructive criticism while maintaining morale and motivation. It consists of three parts: Top Slice, Meat, Bottom Slice.

:::


1. **Top Slice: Positive Feedback**

   
   1. Start with something the person is doing well to create a positive and receptive mindset.

      
      1. Also shows appreciation / recognizes effort to prevent drift
   2. Example: *"You've been doing an excellent job keeping up with your update emails. Your consistency has really helped build trust with our customers."*
2. **Meat: Constructive Feedback**

   
   1. Deliver the area for improvement in a clear, specific, and actionable way.
   2. Example: *"One area that could use improvement is the format / grammar of your emails. They can somewhat casual for a professional email. Let me know if you'd like specific examples on this"*

      
      1. *\*Ask for permission to go into detail, or just leave it with the simple statement & let them figure it out\**
3. **Bottom Slice: Encouraging Closing**

   
   1. End on a positive note to reinforce confidence and motivation.
   2. Example: *"With a few small tweaks, you're updates will be the best in company & you'll be ready for the Team Lead role"*
