Turn Over involves preparing a rental home promptly and efficiently after Resident move-out, ensuring minimal downtime and quick readiness for the next rental cycle.

## Objectives

* Prepare the home for listing as quickly as possible.
* Communicate with and secure approval from the owner.
* Coordinate multiple vendors effectively to prepare the home.
* Optimize the sequence and scheduling of vendor services.


---

## Turn Over: Two-Step Process

Turn Over always follows a two-step dispatch. This structure exists for three key reasons:


1. **The leasing team is not equipped for in-depth diagnostics.** Rent Ready requires specialized assessments — appliance condition evaluation, smoke alarm verification at height, rekeying, CO detector checks — that go beyond what the leasing team is trained to perform. A qualified vendor must handle these items.
2. **Dispatching immediately avoids losing vacancy days.** Rent Ready work must be done regardless of whether the owner or Sagareus manages the turn over. Waiting for the owner's response before dispatching would waste valuable vacancy time.
3. **Rent Ready covers items Sagareus uniquely requires on every property.** Rekeying doors, verifying smoke and CO detectors, checking appliance condition, and cleaning appliances are non-negotiable standards that protect both residents and owners.

For full details on each phase, see:

* [Turn Over — Phase 1: Rent Ready](../../sops/turn over/turn-over-step-1-rent-ready.md) — Immediate dispatch, no owner approval required
* [Turn Over — Phase 2: Turn Over Estimate](../../sops/turn over/turn-over-step-2-turn-over-estimate.md) — Owner approval required based on communication settings


:::info
**Note on terminology:** the two phases above describe the *dispatch strategy* for turn over work. The **Turn Over Asana SOP** below numbers the *operational subtasks* (1–10). "Step 1" in this doc's terminology means the Rent Ready dispatch phase; "Step 1" in the Asana SOP means the Manager Review subtask. Keep them separate.

:::
