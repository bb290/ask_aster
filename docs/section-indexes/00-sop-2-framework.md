Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) are essential to maintaining consistency, efficiency, and compliance across our organization. They serve as the foundation for how we operate, train, and scale by providing step-by-step guidance for recurring tasks and decision-making.

## Objectives


1. **Ensure Consistency** — Standardize how tasks are performed across teams to minimize errors and variability.
2. **Increase Efficiency** — Provide clear, repeatable steps to streamline workflows and reduce wasted time.
3. **Support Training & Onboarding** — Serve as a foundational resource for educating new hires and upskilling team members.
4. **Establish Accountability** — Clarify roles, responsibilities, and performance expectations for all staff.
5. **Enable Continuous Improvement** — Create a framework for evaluating and refining procedures based on feedback and operational needs.
6. **Maintain Compliance & Mitigate Risk** — Ensure all processes align with legal, regulatory, and company standards.

## SOP 2.0 Structure

We maintain two SOP tiers: **Director-Level** and **Employee-Level**. Each serves a different purpose, audience, and access level.

### Director-Level SOPs

**Purpose:** Set the strategic, legal, financial, and operational standards for how Sagareus runs at the highest level.

**Use Cases:**


1. Company financials, budgeting, P&L logic
2. Compensation models, salary bands, bonus structures
3. HR processes: hiring decisions, disciplinary actions, sensitive matters
4. Audit prep, tax compliance, legal risk management
5. High-level workflow architecture
6. Policy creation and exceptions
7. Owner relations strategy and escalation paths

**Audience:** Directors and leadership only. (Contains confidential and sensitive information employees do not need for daily execution.)

**Tone:** High-level, strategic, and policy-driven.

### Employee-Level SOPs

**Purpose:** Provide clear, repeatable, step-by-step instructions so staff can execute their daily responsibilities consistently and accurately.

**Use Cases:**


1. Leasing tasks (advertising, showings, screening, lease execution)
2. Maintenance intake, dispatch, communication, and documentation
3. Accounting workflows as they relate to day-to-day tasks
4. Customer service scripts and response procedures
5. Checklists, templates, screenshots, samples
6. Hand-offs, deadlines, and quality standards
7. KPI Metrics & tracking details

**Audience:** Leasing, Maintenance, Ops, Accounting, Admin, Customer Service, and Field Teams.

**Tone:** Practical, simple, action-oriented. Built for execution.

### How the Two Work Together


1. **Director-Level = Governance.** Defines the rules, strategy, risk boundaries, and "why" behind decisions.
2. **Employee-Level = Execution.** Translates approved processes into straightforward tasks employees can follow without needing sensitive context.


:::info
Director updates flow downward. Employees do not modify SOPs; they provide feedback for potential revisions.

:::

### Revision Rules

**Director-Level SOPs:**


1. Updated only by Directors.
2. Used to guide policy shifts, strategic updates, and sensitive decisions.

**Employee-Level SOPs:**


1. Updated after Director changes are finalized.
2. Must stay aligned with the most recent Director version.

## Email Templates

### Benefits of Email Templates for Staff


1. **Consistency in Communication** — Templates ensure that all staff members deliver uniform, professional messages that reflect the company's tone and values.
2. **Saves Time** — Staff can respond quickly without rewriting the same types of messages, improving efficiency and reducing response time.
3. **Reduces Errors** — Pre-written, approved content helps minimize typos, misinformation, or missed details.
4. **Improves Training & Onboarding** — New team members can get up to speed faster with ready-to-use examples of how to communicate with clients, Residents, or vendors.
5. **Supports Compliance** — Templates can be designed to include legally required language or company policies, reducing risk and ensuring important details aren't left out.
6. **Enhances Customer Experience** — Clear, consistent communication builds trust with recipients and reflects positively on your brand.

### Intention & Tone

When drafting email templates for SOPs, follow these tone and intent standards to ensure clarity, efficiency, and professionalism:

* **Direct and Assertive** — Templates should clearly state the action being taken or recommended, assuming the recipient's agreement unless there is a compelling reason not to.
* **Lead with Confidence** — Our staff are trained professionals empowered to make appropriate recommendations. Templates should reflect that authority and present decisions or next steps as the default course of action.
* **Optimize for Mobile Reading** — Structure emails so they can be quickly scanned and understood on a mobile device. The goal is to make it easy for the recipient to respond with a simple "Looks good" when in agreement.
* **Avoid Passive or Unclear Language** — Phrases like "Let me know what you think" or "Please advise how you'd like us to proceed" should be avoided. These signal indecision and weaken the message. Instead, use language that guides the recipient toward a clear and timely response.

#### Assertive and Action-Oriented Phrases

* "Unless we hear otherwise by \[date\], we'll move forward as outlined below."
* "We'll go ahead with \[recommended action\] unless you indicate a different preference."
* "Let us know if you have any objections by \[deadline\]; otherwise, we'll take care of it."
* "We'll proceed with this plan unless directed otherwise."

#### Use AI to revise / refine

* After you write your first draft, utilize AI tools to revise / refine until you feel the template meets the above guidelines
