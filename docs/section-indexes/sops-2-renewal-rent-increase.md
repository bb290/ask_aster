:::warning
Renewal & Rent Increase are heavily regulated in Washington. Strict state rules govern when and how these can be done, how much rent can increase, and the notice and delivery requirements. Failure to follow law exposes landlords to penalties.

:::

* Renewal protects the owner's right to access their property. Once a lease is signed, it is legally binding and owners cannot regain possession until the lease ends.
* Owners may have future plans for the property (moving in, selling, or needing rental income). Sagareus negotiates lease terms to meet owner requirements.
* Renewal is equally important for Residents. Moving is a significant life event, so the renewal process should be predictable and considerate to avoid unnecessary stress.
* Rent Increase is about maintaining/increasing owner ROI. Property taxes, insurance, and maintenance costs rise each year. Rent needs to keep pace to support ongoing service and property upkeep.
* Renewal & Rent Increase are profit centers for Sagareus; renewal fees are charged to owners for each lease renewal or MTM rent increase.
* Effective management of renewals and rent increases is a core competitive advantage for Sagareus. This process is complex, deadline-driven, paperwork-heavy, and difficult to scale. Our systems and staffing allow us to handle this at volume, unlike smaller competitors.

## Policies


:::warning
* Once a lease becomes MTM, it cannot be converted back to a termed lease. Always renew or terminate a lease to maintain owner flexibility.
  * In WA, landlords cannot terminate MTM leases except for specific legal reasons.
  * Residents can terminate MTM leases at any time, regardless of season.
* All rent increases are added only to the Rent portion of recurring charges. Utilities and other fields remain unchanged.
  * This minimizes scrutiny during evictions.
  * Housing authorities have strict rules about utility charges.

:::

## Lifecycle Overview


:::info
Two main cycles: **Termed Leases** and **Month-to-Month (MTM)** renewals.

MTM renewal follows a subset of the termed lease process. Landlord may raise rent every 12 months.

For termed leases, renewal and rent increases can only occur at the lease end, and only once every 12 months since the last increase.

:::
