:::warning
Government housing programs (Section 8, TBRA, VASH, etc.) are **protected sources of income** under Washington State law ([RCW 59.18.255](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.18.255)). It is **illegal** to deny an applicant based on their participation in any government housing program. Sagareus **always** accepts government housing vouchers.

:::

Government housing programs provide rental subsidies to low-income individuals, families, veterans, and people with disabilities. These programs are administered at the federal, state, and local level through various housing authorities.

* **Section 8 (Housing Choice Voucher)** — The largest federal rental assistance program. Tenants receive a voucher that covers a portion of rent; the tenant pays the remainder. Requires housing authority approval and property inspection.
* **Tenant-Based Rental Assistance (TBRA)** — State/local rental assistance programs that function similarly to Section 8.
* **Rapid Rehousing (RRH)** — Short-term rental assistance to help individuals transition out of homelessness.
* **VA VASH Vouchers** — Rental assistance for homeless veterans, combining HUD vouchers with VA support services.
* **Non-Profit Rental Assistance** — Organizations like Catholic Community Services, Sound Families, Solid Ground, Share & Care Housing provide rental subsidies.

## Policies


:::warning
**Source of Income Protection — Three Layers of Law**

* [**RCW 59.18.255**](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.18.255) — Washington State law prohibits landlords from refusing to rent based on source of income, including Section 8 vouchers
* [**RCW 49.60.222**](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=49.60.222) — Source of income is a protected class under the Washington Law Against Discrimination (WLAD), enforced by the Washington State Human Rights Commission
* [**SMC 14.08**](https://library.municode.com/wa/seattle/codes/municipal_code?nodeId=TIT14_HURI_CH14.08_UNFAM) — Seattle's Fair Housing Ordinance provides additional source of income protections, enforced by the Seattle Office of Civil Rights
* [**KCC 12.20**](https://kingcounty.gov/en/legacy/council/legislation/kc-code/24-title-12) — King County Fair Housing Ordinance also protects source of income
* Violations can result in complaints, civil lawsuits, damages, and attorney fees
* Sagareus **always** accepts Section 8 and all other lawful sources of income — **no exceptions**

:::

## Section 8 — Overview

Section 8 (Housing Choice Voucher) is the most common government housing program Sagareus encounters.

* **What it is:** A federal program that pays a portion of a tenant's rent directly to the landlord. The tenant pays the remaining "tenant portion."
* **Tenancy requires approval** from the local housing authority that issued the voucher — KCHA, SHA, THA, RHA, PCHA, HASCO
* **Property must pass inspection** by the housing authority before move-in (Housing Quality Standards)
* **Process typically takes 3–4 weeks** from application approval to move-in
* **Fair Housing:** It is **illegal** to deny an applicant because they use a Section 8 voucher. When anyone asks if we accept Section 8, the answer is **always yes**.

## Section 8 Applicant Review


1. Section 8 applications are reviewed with the exact same criteria as all applications
2. When prospects ask if we accept Section 8, the answer is always yes (and it's illegal for anyone to deny them based on income source)
3. Section 8 voucher is counted toward total income, contribution to the 2.5x rent requirement
4. Once application is reviewed & approved, we must complete Section 8 specific steps:
   * Leasing Team will continue showing and actively working to lease the property while these steps are being completed.
   * Leasing Team **will not** hold a property for a Section 8 applicant (or any applicant) unless specifically instructed to by the property owner.
