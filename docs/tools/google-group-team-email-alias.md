:::info
Team Emails managed through Google Groups are designed to make everyone's job easier. They give small teams with similar responsibilities a shared workspace where communication is visible, organized, and easy to support collectively.

Instead of messages piling up in individual inboxes, emails live in a shared Group inbox that functions like a work queue. Team members can work through messages together, assign ownership as they go, and step in seamlessly for one another. This results in better collaboration, cleaner handoffs, and real coverage when someone is out of the office.

One of the biggest benefits: time off actually feels like time off. When the team works from a shared queue, no one returns from a few days away to hundreds of unread emails or a frantic game of catch-up. The work kept moving along, a team member can jump right in and relieve their colleague for their days of rest.

:::


:::tip
This SOP exists to help the team:

* Avoid duplicate or crossed responses
* Make ownership clear without micromanagement
* Reduce unnecessary inbox clutter
* Communicate with a consistent, professional company voice

:::

Think of the Group inbox as a shared to-do list, not a group chat. When the system is used as intended, it creates less stress, better coverage, and smoother days for everyone involved.


---

## How to Use Google Groups (Day-to-Day)

Google Groups works best when it's treated like a shared work queue the team manages together.


1. Access Group inbox via Google Group App
2. Review unassigned messages, starting with the oldest & working your way up.
3. Update status and/or Assign the email to yourself once you start working on it

   
   1. Assigning to yourself tells the team: *"I've got this."*
4. Respond via Team Alias to the sender when ready, using templates or prior threads for consistency
5. Once resolved, mark the conversation complete so the queue stays clean and current

If you're out for the day (or a few days), there's nothing special you need to do. The inbox keeps moving, teammates can self-assign messages, and the work continues without piling up in your absence.
