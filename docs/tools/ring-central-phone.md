**Provider:** RingCentral

**Purpose:** To establish consistent standards for handling all inbound and outbound calls through RingCentral, ensuring professional communication, customer satisfaction, and healthy work/life boundaries for staff.


:::warning
RingCentral is the official company phone system. All business communication must occur inside RingCentral (mobile app or desktop app). Personal cell numbers must never be given to tenants, owners, or vendors.

:::

## Continuous Improvement

* Policies & procedures will be updated as the company grows and call volume increases.

### Quality Assurance


:::warning
* Professional tone, courtesy, and adherence to policy are mandatory at all times.
* Failure to use RingCentral as required (e.g., bypassing with personal cell phones, failing to answer, unprofessional tone) will result in corrective action.

:::

* RingCentral provides call recording and reporting. Calls may be monitored or reviewed for training and quality assurance.
* Management will review sample calls weekly to highlight strong performance and identify improvements.

## General Standards

* Every staff member is assigned a dedicated **work phone number** through RingCentral.
* This number must be used for all business calls and texts.
* Personal cell numbers must never be given to tenants, owners, or vendors.
* All business communication must occur inside RingCentral (mobile app or desktop app).

### Business Hours & Coverage


:::info
The main line is answered Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM, 5:00 PM. Staff are not expected to answer calls or texts outside of scheduled shifts unless on-call.

:::

* Each team member has an established work schedule and is expected to answer calls and texts during their shift.
* All work communication is through RingCentral.
* If a call or text can't be answered immediately, it should be returned within **two working hours** whenever possible.
* Calls that come in outside of business hours will route to voicemail or to the on-call staff member for emergencies.
* RingCentral's scheduling features will enforce these boundaries automatically.

### Greetings & Voicemails

**Main Line Greeting:** Standardized script approved by management.

#### Staff Voicemails


:::info
Voicemail greeting must **clearly identify you and the company** so callers know they've reached the right person.

:::

**Required elements:**

* Your name
* Company name ("Sagareus Property Management")
* Simple instruction to leave name, number, property address

**Allowed Examples:**

> **Professional Standard:**
> "Hi, you've reached \[Name\] with Sagareus Property Management. Please leave your name, phone number, and property address, and I'll return your call."

> **Professional + Personality:**
> "Hello, this is \[Name\] with Sagareus Property Management. I may be on another call or out in the field, but I'd love to help you. Please leave your name, number, and property address, and I'll get back to you shortly."

> **Warm + Friendly:**
> "Hi, this is \[Name\] at Sagareus Property Management. Thanks for calling! Leave your name, number, and property address, and I'll be in touch as soon as I can."


:::warning
**Not Allowed:** Overly casual greetings ("Hey it's me"), jokes, background noise, or anything that could be perceived as unprofessional.

:::

## Call Handling

### Answering Calls


:::info
Keep it professional, but simple and friendly. Every caller should immediately know who they reached and who they're speaking with.

:::

**Required elements:**

* Company name ("Sagareus")
* Your name
* Offer of assistance

**Acceptable Scripts:**

> "Hi, this is \[Name\] with Sagareus. How can I help?"
> "Good \[morning/afternoon\], this is \[Name\] with Sagareus. How can I help you today?"
> "Thanks for calling Sagareus, this is \[Name\]. What can I do for you?"


:::warning
**Not Allowed:** Answering with just "Hello," skipping the company name, or overly casual responses.

:::

### Hold / Transfers


:::info
Sometimes a caller needs to speak with another team member or department. How you handle that transition is just as important as answering the call. The goal is to make sure the caller feels taken care of, not "passed around."

:::

#### Place a Caller on Hold

* **Always ask permission first.** Never drop someone on hold without notice.
* Let them know it will be brief and thank them for their patience.

**Acceptable Scripts:**

> "Would you mind holding for a quick moment while I connect you with the right person?"
> "I'll need just a moment to pull up your information, may I place you on hold?"

#### Warm Transfer (Preferred Standard)


:::tip
A **warm transfer** means you speak to the receiving staff member before passing the call, so they know who's calling and why. This is the Sagareus standard because it ensures a smooth, professional handoff and prevents the caller from having to explain the reason for their call more than once.

:::

**Steps:**


1. Place caller on hold.
2. Call the receiving staff member, introduce the caller, and explain the reason for transfer.
   * "Hi \[Name\], I have \[Caller's Name\] on the line regarding \[topic\]. Can you take the call?"
3. If the receiving staff member agrees, connect the caller.
   * You are now on a 3-way call.
   * Introduce caller to receiving staff member:
     * "Hi \[Caller\], thank you for holding. I have \[staff member\] on the line for you, he/she will assist you with \[topic\] from here."

#### If Warm Transfer Fails

If the receiving staff member doesn't answer:

* Return to the caller (don't leave them hanging).
* Offer to route them to voicemail or the Main Line for customer service.

**Acceptable Script:**

> "Thanks for holding. \[Name\] isn't available right now. I can route you to their voicemail, or if you'd like immediate assistance, I can transfer you to our customer service team to submit a ticket."
