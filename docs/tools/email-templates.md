## Email Etiquette Basics

Staff frequently interact with prospective and current Residents, property owners, and vendors, making professionalism and clarity key in every email.


:::tip
**Best practices for maintaining professional email etiquette:**


 1. Clear subject lines
 2. Professional greetings
 3. Solution-oriented tone
 4. Brief and scannable
 5. Proofread before sending
 6. Professional signature
 7. Respond within 24 hours
 8. No ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
 9. Use CC/BCC appropriately
10. Professional closing

:::


 1. **Use a Clear and Concise Subject Line**
    * Example: **"Lease Renewal Reminder, Response Requested"**
    * Avoid vague subjects like **"Important"** or **"Question"**
 2. **Greet the Recipient Professionally**
    * Use proper salutations:
      * **Formal:** *Mr. Smith,*
      * **Less formal but professional:** *Hello Sarah, Hi Sarah*
      * **Avoid overly casual greetings like:** *Hey* or *Hiya*
 3. **Keep the Tone Professional and Friendly**
    * Be polite, respectful, and direct.
    * Use positive and solution-oriented language.
 4. **Keep It Brief and to the Point**
    * Use short paragraphs and **bullet points for readability**.
    * If the email is lengthy, consider an attachment or a phone call
 5. **Proofread Before Sending**
    * Check for typos, grammatical errors, and clarity.
    * Ensure all necessary attachments are included.
 6. **Use a Professional Email Signature**, including:
    * Your full name
    * Job title (e.g., *Leasing Consultant | ABC Property Management*)
    * Company name and contact details
    * Company logo (if applicable)
 7. **Respond Promptly and Follow Up When Necessary**
    * Aim to reply within **24 hours** during business days.
    * If more time is needed, acknowledge receipt and provide an estimated response time.
 8. **Avoid Using All Caps or Excessive Punctuation**
    * Writing in all caps = **SHOUTING**
    * Too many exclamation marks can seem unprofessional (**e.g., "Thanks!!!"**)
 9. **Use CC and BCC Appropriately**
    * **CC (Carbon Copy):** Use when others need to be informed but don't need to respond.
    * **BCC (Blind Carbon Copy):** Use for mass emails to protect recipients' privacy.
10. **End with a Professional Closing**

## Advanced Email Etiquette, Owner Specific Communication


:::warning
Everything you say will be scrutinized, so double check your response for accuracy.

**NO AI slop policy**, Employees are encouraged to use AI to help review tone, draft content, improve writing. **BUT**, avoid copy and pasting AI content directly from prompt to email without review. AI responses tend to be wordy and have the wrong tone by default. It will require several prompts before you land on the right response.

:::

* Owners hired Sagareus to reclaim their time
  * **We should not trouble the owner for ANY information that we could have verified independently without their input.**
* **BLUF, bottom line up front**, the first sentence in the email should be a question or action item for the owner.
* **Avoid unnecessary pleasantries, every word in the email should meaningfully contribute to the message you want to convey.**
* **Provide all the context for recipients to make decisions.** Assume the recipients are busy, they don't know the backstories. Provide all the context for them to make decisions so owners know where/what/why this issue is being sent now.
* Always include picture/video to support an issue, **image/video should be shared via a Google Drive instead of attached to a file directly**


:::info
**Email subject should be searchable, always include the address in the subject:**

This applies to Asana tasks as well, all tasks should have address in the title so they are searchable.

Format: **\[Call to Action\] // \[Address\]**

:::

### Templated Response Policy


:::warning
Templated responses are the **company-wide standard** for written communication. While certain situations may require slight personalization, the **core message, structure, and tone** must remain aligned with the original template. This ensures:

* Consistency across departments
* Fair and equitable treatment of all stakeholders
* Efficiency in staff training and response times
* Legal and procedural compliance

:::

**Use Templates First**

Where available, Staff must begin with an approved template when responding to Resident, owner, or vendor inquiries. Personalization may be added to:

* Acknowledge specific details or context
* Add warmth or empathy
* Clarify information where needed

**Do Not Deviate from Core Messaging**

Altering the intent, tone, or structure of a message to "improvise" or "reframe" beyond approved edits is discouraged and may result in inconsistent or non-compliant communication.

**Review and Maintain SOP Alignment**

All communication must align with the current Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). Staff should regularly refer to the SOP and flag any discrepancies between templates and updated policies.


:::tip
**Our Goal:** 100% of recurring or foreseeable communications should have a templated response. This reduces mental load, improves customer experience, provides standardized experience across the organization and allows Sagareus to scale while maintaining quality and compliance.
