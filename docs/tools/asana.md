**The System of Record & Execution Framework.**

Asana is the central nervous system of Sagareus. If it's not in Asana, it doesn't exist.


:::warning
This SOP establishes Asana as the **single source of truth** for task management, accountability, deadlines, and cross-department visibility. It defines how Asana is structured, how it must be used, and the standards required to ensure consistency, compliance, and scalability across the company.

:::

Our business operates across leasing, maintenance, accounting, and customer service; Often simultaneously. Asana allows us to replace memory, inboxes, and side conversations with a clear, auditable system that ensures:

* Work is assigned intentionally
* Deadlines are visible and enforced
* Ownership is clear
* Progress is trackable in real time
* Institutional knowledge lives in the system, not in people's heads

Following this SOP protects Sagareus from:

* Missed deadlines
* Duplicated work
* Compliance gaps
* "I thought someone else was handling it" failures
* Operational bottlenecks that don't scale past today's workload


:::tip
**The goal is simple: Clarity over chaos. Systems over heroics. Execution over noise.**

Every team member is expected to understand and follow this SOP exactly as written. Deviations, workarounds, or parallel systems are not permitted unless explicitly approved.

As Sagareus grows, Asana is how we grow *without breaking*.

:::

## Internal Communication Standard

All non-urgent, work-related communication between team members must occur directly within the relevant Asana task or subtask.


:::info
This standard exists to:

* Reduce internal email volume
* Eliminate fragmented conversations
* Preserve context
* Improve transparency
* Ensure accountability across teams
* Allow work to be visible, searchable, and auditable

:::


:::warning
Email is reserved for **external communication**. Asana is for **internal execution.**

:::

### Asana > email

When communication lives in inboxes:

* Context gets lost
* Only part of the team sees the conversation
* Decisions are hard to track
* Work becomes dependent on who was CC'd

When communication lives in Asana:

* The entire team can see progress in real time
* Conversations stay attached to the actual work
* History, decisions, and next steps are preserved
* We eliminate "9000 emails to everyone's inbox"

### Compliance & Enforcement


:::warning
Failure to follow internal communication standards:

* Slows execution
* Reduces visibility
* Creates operational risk
* Undermines team efficiency

Repeated failure to use Asana for internal communication may result in poor performance feedback or corrective action. This standard is a baseline expectation for all roles.

:::

### Communication Methods in Asana

#### Mentions (@Name) = FYI / Context Only

Use **mentions** when:

* Sharing information
* Providing background or visibility
* Flagging something for awareness
* Looping someone in without requiring action

Mentions **do not** require a response


:::tip
**Mentions = Awareness, not action.**

:::

#### Assigned Subtasks = Action Required

[**Video Overview**](https://www.loom.com/share/7a42e508d028464a88a886a9de649b37?sid=7cc2ae12-064e-44f5-9c4f-f07cfcef2306)

If another team member needs to do **anything**, even something small; Create a **subtask**, assign it to them, and include a **due date**.

This includes:

* Fulfilling a maintenance request
* Reviewing something / Providing input / Confirming next steps
* Responding to a question
* Reaching out / following up with someone


:::warning
**If action is required, a subtask is required.**

:::


:::info
Assigned subtasks communicate:

* Who owns the action
* What action is needed
* When it is due

No guessing. No chasing. No "I thought you were just looping me in."

:::

#### Due Dates Are Necessary

Due dates ensure the assignee actually sees the task:

* Everyone operates from the "My Tasks" page within Asana
* Filtered by due date.
* No date = It doesn't even show up.

If timing is flexible, choose a reasonable default and adjust if needed.

### Asana = Where Internal Communication Belongs


:::info
* If it impacts execution, it belongs in Asana.
* All internal discussion must occur within:
  * The relevant **Parent Task**, or
  * A clearly labeled **Subtask**
* Do not start internal conversations in email if the work already exists in Asana
* If an internal email is received:
  * Move the conversation into Asana
  * Reference it there
  * Continue discussion in the task going forward

:::

#### Back-and-Forth = Expected and Encouraged

Yes, sometimes subtasks get reassigned back and forth with comments; Similar to an email thread. That's **intentional**, not a failure.

The difference:

* The conversation is attached to the actual work
* All related notes, files, and context live in one place
* The entire team has visibility without inbox overload

This structure allows complex issues to evolve **without chaos**.

#### Internal Emails = What NOT to Use

Do **not** use:

* Email chains for internal coordination
* Text messages for non-urgent work discussion
* Side conversations that affect task outcomes
* "Verbal-only" updates with no Asana record
* If an internal email is received:
  * Move the conversation into Asana
  * Reference it there
  * Continue discussion in the task going forward

### Exceptions

* True emergencies
* Time-sensitive, safety-related issues
* System outages preventing Asana access

Even in these cases, summarize the communication in Asana afterward.

## Internal Documentation Standard


:::warning
**If it's not documented, it didn't happen.**

All Sagareus staff across every department are required to document every business-related communication in Asana. This includes all attempts to communicate, whether successful or not.

:::

Documentation is not busywork. It is how we ensure **accountability, transparency, continuity, legal protection, and scalability**.

### Compliance & Enforcement


:::warning
Failure to document communication as outlined:

* Creates operational risk
* Increases legal exposure
* Impacts performance evaluations

While mistakes happen, repeated failure to document is grounds for termination. Consistent documentation is a baseline expectation across all roles at Sagareus.

:::

### What Must Be Documented

All verbal or written business communication must be logged in Asana, including but not limited to:

* Conversations (phone, in person, video)
* Emails and texts
* Decisions and approvals
* Commitments and timelines
* Agreements and disputes
* Status updates and follow-ups
* Missed or unreturned contact attempts


:::warning
If communication impacts **money, deadlines, habitability, compliance, or client expectations**, it must be documented.

:::

### Why Must Everything be Documented

#### Team Awareness & Accountability

Up-to-date comments and communication logs allow the entire team to confirm that work is actively progressing.

When documentation is missing, it creates the appearance that **no action has been taken**, even if work occurred. Clear records allow Sagareus to confidently respond to owners or Residents with statements such as: "Yes, we've reached out multiple times and are currently awaiting a response."

Visibility protects both the company **and** the individual team member.

### Improves Scheduling & Response Success


:::info
Documenting outreach attempts helps identify response patterns. For example:

* If messages sent Wednesdays at noon go unanswered
* Adjusting outreach to Tuesday mornings or Saturday afternoons may yield better results

Tracking these details increases successful contact rates and reduces repeated, ineffective outreach.

:::

### Legal Protection & Compliance Support

If a contact becomes unresponsive and Sagareus must proceed with:

* 48-Hour Entry Notices
* 10-Day Notices to Comply or Vacate
* Demand Letters
* Other legal or compliance actions


:::warning
Well-documented, good-faith communication attempts are critical. Clear records protect the company and strengthen our legal position.

:::

### Performance Tracking & Operational Improvement

Consistent documentation provides an objective record for:

* Performance coaching
* Process audits
* Training refinement
* Risk management
* Continuous improvement

If work cannot be reviewed, it cannot be improved.

### When to Document = Always.

* After **every** phone call, text, email, or in-person conversation
* When commitments, timelines, or decisions are discussed
* Following disputes, complaints, or service failures
* When enforcing or explaining policies
* Anytime money, liability, or deadlines are involved
* Before transferring responsibility to another team member

### Where to Document = Asana

* **Leasing, Operations, and Accounting Teams:** Document all communication directly in Asana under the appropriate **Parent Task**
* **When unclear where to document:** Create an Asana task, add title for searchability; Organize later

### How to Document

Documentation should be:

* Factual and neutral
* Timestamped (Asana handles this automatically)
* Clear and concise
* Free of assumptions or personal opinions
* Inclusive of names, dates, decisions, and next steps
* Accompanied by photos when reporting maintenance concerns

#### Best Documentation = Gold Standard


:::tip
The **best** documentation is:

* Screenshots of actual emails or messages
* Downloaded PDFs of sent communications
* Files that clearly show **what**, **when**, and **how** something was communicated

These records are time-stamped, objective, and indisputable. Assume the audience is a judge and jury. You may have done everything right but **can you prove it?**

:::


:::warning
Best documentation is mandatory for matters involving:

* Money
* Deadlines
* Compliance
* Fair Housing
* Perceived preferential treatment

:::

#### Good Documentation = Acceptable

Good documentation consists of brief, informal notes in your own words summarizing actions taken. Think of the audience as Your future self, a teammate stepping in and/or a manager reviewing progress to update client.

Examples:

* "Emailed Resident to schedule access."
* "Texted vendor for availability."
* "Spoke with Amy, out of town until the 10th. Follow up after return."

Asana automatically timestamps all entries.
