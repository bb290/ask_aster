## FLSA Classification of Property Field Services Assistant

### Purpose

To establish the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) classification of the Property Field Services Assistant (Entry Level) position and to ensure compliance with federal and Washington State wage and hour laws.

### Background


:::warning
In Washington (2025), the salary threshold for exemption is **$1,332.80 per week ($69,305.60 annually)**.

:::

* Under the FLSA and Washington law, employees are presumed **Non-Exempt (hourly, overtime-eligible)** unless they meet both the **salary threshold** and the **duties test** for exemption.
* In Washington (2025), the salary threshold is **$1,332.80 per week ($69,305.60 annually)**.
* Common exemptions include Executive, Administrative, Professional, Computer, and Outside Sales.

### Scope of Duties for Property Field Services Assistant

Property Field Services Assistant responsibilities include:

#### Job Duties Summary

* Conduct move-out condition reports, annual inspections, and incident reports with photos and notes.
* Post legal notices, check on vacant units, deliver/retrieve items, and monitor property conditions.
* Install/remove lockboxes, meet vendors, and provide unit access.
* Respond to incidents (e.g., water intrusion, odor, pests, mold) and install temporary equipment as directed.
* Maintain accurate logs, track mileage, and submit documentation of fieldwork.
* Participate in weekly team syncs and communicate field observations.

### FLSA Classification Analysis

#### 1. Salary Basis Test

* This role is **hourly paid ($23–$28/hr DOE)**.
* Does **not** meet the salary basis threshold for exemption under Washington State law ($69,305.60/year in 2025).

#### 2. Duties Test

* Primary duties involve **field inspections, documentation, driving, and following established procedures**.
* Work is **task-oriented** and directed by supervisors; does not include independent discretion or decision-making of significant business matters.
* No authority over hiring, firing, or policy-level decisions.

**Conclusion:** The role **does not meet the executive, administrative, or professional exemption criteria**.

### Classification

**Property Field Services Assistant is classified as NON-EXEMPT under the FLSA and Washington State law.**

* Eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x the regular hourly rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek.
* Must record all hours worked (including travel between properties, incident response, and administrative reporting time).
* Compensable time includes travel between job sites and performing work-related duties offsite.
* Commute from home to the first site and from the last site back home is **not** compensable, unless directed otherwise by management.

### Timekeeping Expectations

* Employees must clock in/out daily via ADP timekeeping system.
* All work performed outside normal hours (calls, texts, emails, posting notices, or after-hours vendor access) must be recorded as worked time.
* Mileage logs must be submitted weekly for reimbursement.

### Compliance

Managers and supervisors are responsible for:

* Monitoring employee time records for accuracy.
* Ensuring overtime is pre-approved where possible.
* Providing training on compensable time rules for travel, fieldwork, and incidental tasks.
