## FLSA Classification of Leasing Agents

### Purpose

To establish consistent criteria for determining the proper classification of Leasing Agents under the **Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)** and Washington State wage and hour laws. This ensures compliance, minimizes legal risk, and promotes fair pay practices.

### Background


:::warning
In Washington (2025), the salary threshold for exemption is **$1,332.80 per week ($69,305.60 annually)**.

:::

* Under the FLSA and Washington law, employees are presumed **Non-Exempt (hourly, overtime-eligible)** unless they meet both the **salary threshold** and the **duties test** for exemption.
* In Washington (2025), the salary threshold is **$1,332.80 per week ($69,305.60 annually)**.
* Common exemptions include Executive, Administrative, Professional, Computer, and Outside Sales.

### Scope of Duties for Leasing Agents

Typical Leasing Agent responsibilities include:

* Serving as the primary contact for prospective tenants.
* Conducting property tours, responding to leasing inquiries, and explaining lease terms.
* Completing leasing-related documentation such as applications, lease agreements, and move-in/move-out condition reports.
* Coordinating move-in logistics, including inspections, key handoffs, and rent collection.
* Providing weekly activity updates to property owners and management.
* Executing established leasing procedures while **following company policies and regulatory guidelines**.

This scope emphasizes the **execution of leasing activities** and **customer service**, rather than independent policy-making or managerial authority.

### Classification

Leasing Agent is classified as NON-EXEMPT under the FLSA and Washington State law.

* Eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x the regular hourly rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek.
* All hours worked must be accurately recorded in the timekeeping system.
* Compensable time includes after-hours calls, emails, property showings, and required travel between properties.

### Conclusion

Leasing Agents should be classified as **Non-Exempt (Hourly, OT-eligible)** unless the position is structured to clearly meet the **Outside Sales Exemption** requirements.

### Timekeeping Expectations

* Employees must clock in/out daily via ADP timekeeping system.
* All work performed outside normal hours (calls, texts, emails, posting notices, or after-hours vendor access) must be recorded as worked time.
* Mileage logs must be submitted weekly for reimbursement.

### Compliance

Managers and supervisors are responsible for:

* Monitoring employee time records for accuracy.
* Ensuring overtime is pre-approved where possible.
* Providing training on compensable time rules for travel, fieldwork, and incidental tasks.
