## FLSA Classification of Assistant Property Manager

### 1. Salary Basis Test


:::warning
If paid salary, the role must meet the Washington State minimum salary threshold for exemption in 2025 (**$1,332.80/week or $69,305.60/year**). If paid hourly, the role is automatically non-exempt.

:::

* If paid hourly: Automatically **non-exempt**.
* If paid salary: Must meet Washington State minimum salary threshold for exemption in 2025 ($1,332.80/week or $69,305.60/year).

### 2. Duties Test

* Primary duties involve **supporting property management operations**, applying established procedures, and following company policies.
* Work is **largely transactional and administrative**, with limited discretion on matters of significance.
* Does not have authority to make independent policy decisions, set budgets, or hire/fire employees.
* Reports to the Property Manager and acts in a support capacity.

**Conclusion:** The role does **not meet executive, administrative, or professional exemption criteria.**

### Classification

**Assistant Property Manager is classified as NON-EXEMPT under the FLSA and Washington State law.**

* Eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x the regular hourly rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek.
* All hours worked must be accurately recorded in the timekeeping system.
* Compensable time includes resident communications, after-hours calls, property inspections, and preparation of reports if performed outside of scheduled hours.

### Timekeeping Expectations

* Employees must clock in/out daily via ADP timekeeping system.
* Time spent responding to work-related calls, texts, or emails after hours must be recorded as worked time.
* Travel between properties during the workday is compensable; normal commute to/from home is not.

### Compliance

Supervisors and managers are responsible for:

* Reviewing time records for accuracy.
* Approving overtime as needed.
* Training staff on compensable time rules and company procedures.
