To provide a standardized process for determining and documenting employee classification as **exempt** or **non-exempt** under Washington State and federal wage and hour laws.

This SOP applies to all employees of Sagareus and is used by HR, payroll, and management to ensure compliance with salary thresholds, duties tests, and exemption criteria.

## Definitions

* **Exempt Employee:** An employee not eligible for overtime pay, meeting minimum salary thresholds and the duties test for executive, administrative, professional, computer professional, or outside sales roles.
* **Non-Exempt Employee:** An employee eligible for overtime pay for hours worked over 40 in a workweek.

## Salary Thresholds for Exempt Employees (2025)


:::warning
Salary thresholds below are the statutory minimums for Washington State (small employers, 1–50 employees) in 2025. Paying the minimum salary alone does not guarantee exemption — the duties test must also be met.

:::

| Exemption Type | Minimum Salary Requirement (Small Employers: 1-50 employees) |
|:--------------:|:------------------------------------------------------------:|
| Most White-Collar Exempt Employees (Executive, Administrative, Professional) | $1,332.80/week ($69,305.60/year)                             |
| Computer Professionals (Exempt) | $1,332.80/week **OR** $58.31/hour (whichever applies)        |
| Outside Sales  | No minimum salary; exempt by nature of duties                |

**Notes:**

* Exempt status **requires meeting the duties test**; paying the minimum salary alone does not guarantee exemption.
* Salary thresholds are updated annually based on state minimum wage and CPI.

## Job Duties Test

**The employee's primary job duties, not title or description, determine exemption status.**

## Exempt Categories & Criteria


1. **Executive Exemption**
   * Primary duty: Managing the business or a department.
   * Directs work of two or more employees.
   * Significant input on personnel decisions (hiring, firing, promotions).
2. **Administrative Exemption**
   * Primary duty: Office/non-manual work directly related to management or general business operations.
   * Exercises discretion and independent judgment on matters of significance.
3. **Professional Exemption**
   * Primary duty: Requires advanced knowledge in a field of science or learning (e.g., law, medicine, engineering).
   * Knowledge typically acquired through specialized intellectual instruction.
4. **Computer Professional Exemption**
   * Primary duty: Provides computer-related services (system analysis, programming, software engineering, etc.).
   * Must meet specific criteria for tasks, knowledge, and skills.
5. **Outside Sales Exemption**
   * Primary duty: Making sales or obtaining orders.
   * Sales duties are the employee's main job.

## Key Points

* Job duties, not job title, determine exemption status.
* Employees must meet **all three tests** (salary threshold, duties test, and salary basis) to qualify as exempt.
* Non-exempt employees are entitled to **overtime pay** (1.5x) for hours over 40/week.
* HR and managers are responsible for accurately assessing and documenting each employee's classification.

## Procedure


1. **Job Evaluation**
   * HR reviews job description and primary duties.
   * Compare duties against exempt categories and tests.
2. **Salary Verification**
   * Confirm employee meets WA 2025 (and each year going forward) salary thresholds or hourly equivalent for computer professionals.
   * Document salary level and calculation.
3. **Classification Decision**
   * If all criteria met → classify as **Exempt**.
   * If criteria not met → classify as **Non-Exempt**.
4. **Documentation**
   * Record classification in HRIS/payroll system.
   * Maintain a copy of job duties assessment and classification rationale.
5. **Review & Updates**
   * Annually review exempt thresholds and duties for compliance with WA law.
   * Update job classifications as duties or salaries change.
