# Decision — Every Unit Must Be Labeled

## Decision

Every unit on every Sagareus-managed property must be physically labeled with its designated unit identifier (e.g., "A", "B", "102") that matches what's on the lease. Confirmation of label presence is a required step on the move-in checklist.

## Why this decision

The 13337 30th Ave NE incident demonstrated that without physical unit labels, neither field staff nor tenants can verify which physical space corresponds to a given lease — especially when (a) coverage is last-minute, (b) the staff member is unfamiliar with the property, or (c) the tenant is moving sight-unseen. The error cost a lease renegotiation in this case; in other configurations it could cost a tenant moving into a unit owned by a different owner, or a unit that's already occupied.

## What this changes operationally

- **Onboarding:** confirm or install unit labels at intake. Onboarding is not considered complete until labels are physically on the units and match the lease designations on file.
- **Move-in:** the move-in checklist gains a "labels match lease designation" verification step. If labels are missing or mismatched, escalate before completing move-in.
- **Audit pass:** existing managed properties without conforming labels need a remediation sweep — install labels at the next maintenance visit or schedule a dedicated labeling round.

## Scope

Applies to all property types with more than one unit on a parcel: multi-family, ADUs on shared lots, duplexes, etc. Single-family homes are exempt (the address itself is the identifier).
