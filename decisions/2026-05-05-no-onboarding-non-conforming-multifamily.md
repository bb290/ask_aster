# Decision — No New Non-Conforming Multifamily Onboarding

## Decision

Effective May 5, 2026, Sagareus no longer onboards non-conforming multifamily properties. Properties currently under management stay under management. No new non-conforming multifamily properties will be signed.

## Why this decision

Non-conforming multifamily — buildings where unit designations aren't permitted, recorded, or physically distinguishable in standard ways — generate a recurring class of operational risk:

- Lease confusion (which unit is leased to whom)
- Mail / utility confusion (which utility account belongs to which unit)
- Move-in confusion (which physical space corresponds to a lease, as the 13337 30th Ave NE incident demonstrated)
- Rent collection ambiguity if multiple tenants share addressing
- Legal exposure if the units' non-conforming status is challenged

Each individual issue is solvable case-by-case, but the aggregate risk profile across the property class is larger than the management revenue justifies.

## What this changes operationally

- **Intake / lead qualification:** at first contact with a prospective owner, confirm whether the property is conforming. Non-conforming multifamily is now a disqualifying criterion for new accounts.
- **Sales:** stop pursuing non-conforming multifamily prospects. Don't tour, don't quote, don't present.
- **Existing management:** no change. Continue managing currently-onboarded non-conforming multifamily properties at our existing service level.

## Scope

- Applies to **new** property onboarding only.
- "Non-conforming" here means properties where the unit configuration is not permitted as multi-family by local zoning, or where unit boundaries / counts are ambiguous in legal records.
- Does not affect ADUs that are individually permitted, conforming duplexes, or properly-zoned multi-family.
