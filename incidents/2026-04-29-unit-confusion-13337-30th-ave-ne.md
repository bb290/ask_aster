# Incident — Unauthorized Unit Transfer at Move-In, 13337 30th Ave NE

## Summary

Tenant relocating from 2301 NE 125th St Unit 102 to 13337 30th Ave NE Unit B was inadvertently moved into Unit A — a meaningfully nicer unit with market rent $400–600/month higher than what the tenant had agreed to. The error was caught after the fact and the lease has been renegotiated. Unit A lease pending signature.

This is the same tenant who had been living in Unit 102 at 2301 NE 125th, where there was an unresolved rat infestation (see related incident).

> Note: exact move-in date is approximate (April 2026); verify and correct `incident_date` in frontmatter if known.

## What happened

- The original lease was for 13337 30th Ave NE Unit B.
- The move-in was covered last-minute by Jessica because Keeanna was double-booked.
- Jessica had never been to the property before the move-in.
- The tenant was moving in sight-unseen.
- The property is a **non-conforming multi-family building** with **no unit labels** — there was no way for either party to confirm which physical unit corresponded to "B" vs "A."
- Tenant moved into Unit A instead of Unit B.
- Unit A is a meaningfully nicer space with market rent $400–600/month higher.

## Resolution

Lease has been renegotiated. The tenant will stay in Unit A at +$500/month over the originally agreed rent.

Unit A lease pending signature with the following terms:

- Rent: $2,650
- Utilities: $225
- Pet Rent: $50
- Deposit: $2,000
- Prorated 1st month: $585

Note: the security deposit had already been transferred from 2301 NE 125th Unit 102 to 13337 30th Ave NE Unit B prior to move-in; that transfer needs to be re-applied to the new Unit A lease.

## Key Issues Impacting Resolution

### 1. Last-minute coverage without property familiarity
Jessica covered Keeanna's move-in last-minute and had never visited the property before. Familiarity with the unit layout would have surfaced the mismatch in time.

### 2. No unit labels
The single biggest preventable factor. When the units aren't physically labeled, there's no in-the-moment verification path — neither staff nor tenant can confirm "this is Unit B" vs "this is Unit A."

### 3. Sight-unseen tenancy
Tenant accepted the unit without visiting. They had no expectation of which physical space was theirs and accepted whichever they were shown.

### 4. Non-conforming multifamily structural risk
Buildings without conforming unit designations create a recurring class of operational risk: mail, lease, utility, and now move-in confusion. This incident is one symptom; we should expect more from this property class generally.

## Current Status

Lease renegotiated; awaiting signature. Tenant currently occupying Unit A. Security deposit re-application pending.

## Follow-Up & Process Improvement

> *These items represent decisions and SOP changes triggered by this incident. They are tracked manually in the Roll Out Asana project under the "Decisions / SOP updates" section.*

### 1. Every unit must be labeled

Before any move-in (and ideally at onboarding), every unit on a property must have clear physical labels matching the leased designation (e.g., "A", "B", "102"). Field tech / move-in coordinator confirms label presence as part of the move-in checklist.

### 2. Sagareus stops onboarding non-conforming multifamily properties

Effective immediately, Sagareus no longer accepts non-conforming multifamily properties under management. Existing managed properties stay under management; no additional non-conforming multifamily properties will be signed.
