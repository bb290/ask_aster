# Incident — Rat Infestation, 2301 NE 125th St Unit 102

## Summary

On January 19, 2026, tenant reported an active rat infestation, including droppings inside the unit and noises in walls. This constitutes a potential habitability concern requiring urgent response.

Despite initial action to engage a pest control vendor, resolution was significantly delayed due to vendor coordination challenges, owner-directed changes in service providers, and lack of timely owner authorization for recommended remediation work.

As of early March 2026, the issue remained unresolved pending owner approval for exclusion and remediation work.

## Timeline

### January 19, 2026
- Tenant reports rat infestation (droppings inside unit).
- Maintenance task created; vendor contacted same day.
- Tenant notified of next steps.

### January 20 – January 28, 2026
- Vendor advises full-property inspection required (multiple units, crawlspace, exterior).
- Coordination required with multiple tenants, causing scheduling delays.
- Earliest vendor availability identified for early February.

### January 29 – January 30, 2026
- Pest control and cleaning vendors initially dispatched.
- Owner instructs cancellation of dispatched vendors and directs use of their preferred vendor.
- Work halted pending owner vendor coordination.

### February 1 – February 10, 2026
- Ongoing follow-up with owner and vendor; delays in scheduling service.
- Tenant continues to report active infestation.
- **February 10:**
  - Pest control report received.
  - Interior cleaning/sanitation dispatched.
  - Tenant credited $250 for inconvenience.

### February 10 – February 24, 2026
- Additional work identified:
  - Exclusion and clean-out
  - Exterior bait stations
- Vendor requires additional inspection to finalize scope and pricing.
- Continued coordination between tenant, vendor, and owner.

### February 25, 2026
- Estimate and scope of work submitted to owner for approval.

### Late February – Early March 2026
- No timely response from owner regarding approval of recommended remediation work.
- Tenant continues to report ongoing rat activity.
- Management continues follow-up attempts with the owner.

## Actions Taken by Management

- Responded to tenant report same day.
- Initiated vendor outreach and scheduling.
- Coordinated access across multiple units.
- Communicated regularly with tenants regarding status updates.
- Dispatched cleaning vendor for interior sanitation.
- Obtained pest control inspection and report.
- Secured estimates for required remediation work.
- Provided tenant rent credit ($250) for inconvenience.
- Maintained ongoing follow-up with owner and vendors.

## Key Issues Impacting Resolution

### 1. Owner-Directed Vendor Change
- Initial vendors were canceled after dispatch.
- Transition to owner's preferred vendor caused delays and restart of process.

### 2. Multi-Unit Coordination Requirements
- Inspection required access to multiple units and structural areas.
- Scheduling complexity contributed to early delays.

### 3. Delayed Owner Authorization
- Recommended remediation work required owner approval.
- Lack of timely response resulted in extended delay.

### 4. Additional Inspection Requirements
- Vendor required follow-up inspection to finalize scope, further extending timeline.

## Follow-Up & Process Improvement

> *These items represent decisions and SOP changes triggered by this incident. They should be moved to `decisions/` once finalized, and the affected SOPs in `sops/` should be updated.*

### 1. Reclassify rodent infestations with interior evidence as Urgent Maintenance

When evidence of infestation is found inside a unit (droppings, active sightings, sounds in walls), classify as **Urgent Maintenance**.

Under company SOP, Urgent Maintenance requests require:
- Assigned Field Tech for daily review
- Same-day or next-day:
  - Property inspection
  - Vendor dispatch
- Reduced dependency on prolonged approval cycles where habitability is at risk
- Decisive follow-up intervals until resolution is confirmed
- Authority to override owner delays under Property Conditions Policy

**Intent:** faster response times, clearer ownership, improved outcomes for future incidents.

### 2. Retrain on Escalation Protocol

Any tenant request to speak with a "manager," "supervisor," or "corporate" is to be escalated through a structured process:
- Respond with a standardized email
- Provide a scheduling link for direct communication with **Brittany F., Designated Broker / Leadership**
