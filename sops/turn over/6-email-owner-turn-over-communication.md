## Compiling the Pending Estimates


:::warning
Presenting large estimates is a negative interaction for the owner. Demonstrate that you took care to minimize cost and are making reasonable recommendations as a good steward of their funds.

:::

**General Principles**

* Be mindful of the rental's quality tier — avoid recommending cosmetic upgrades unless absolutely needed
* Do not recommend countertop replacement unless the existing surface is damaged beyond recovery
* Frame recommendations around protecting the owner's investment, not maximizing upgrades

**Appliances**

* Recommend replacement if repair cost exceeds **45% of replacement value**
* See [Appliance Response](../maintenance/appliance-response.md) for brand recommendations

**Flooring — Old Carpet**

| Condition | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| No odor, no frill, no crushed fiber | Deep cleaning — avoid replacement |
| Has odor, frill, crushed fiber, or significant stains (small unit: studio, 1bd, 2bd, no stairs) | Replace with **LVP** |
| Has odor, frill, crushed fiber, or significant stains (large home or has stairs) | Replace with **new carpet** |


:::info
Recommend carpet over LVP for large homes or homes with stairs. LVP flooring in large properties can easily reach $7,000–$12,000, making carpet the more cost-effective choice.

:::

## Owner Managed Turn Over

### Email Template — Turn Over Plan (Owner Managed)

*Reply all to the move-out notification*

> **Subject:** (keep original subject from move-out notification)
> 
> Hi \[Owner Name\],
> 
> This property is tagged as Owner Managed Turn Over. Unless I hear otherwise, I will assume you will be coordinating all aspects of turn over work.
> 
> **Deposit Withholding**
> 
> • Please provide an itemized invoice or estimate for security deposit withholding **no later than \[25 Days from Move-Out\]** to allow ample processing time.
> • Sagareus must send the previous resident's security deposit statement within 30 days of move out.
> • Missing this deadline could result in a full deposit refund to residents (regardless of damages).
> 
> Note: Only repairs / cleaning that exceed [normal wear and tear](https://www.sagareus.com/blog/what-is-normal-wear-and-tear) may be withheld.
> 
> Thank you,
