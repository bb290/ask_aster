> **Situation:** A resident whose rent is paid by a housing assistance program requests a refund for alleged overpayment.


---

## Key Points

* **Refund to agency only** — When 100% of payments came from a housing assistance program, refunds can only be returned to the issuing agency.
* **Written consent required** — Written consent from the housing authority is required before any credit balance from their funds can be issued directly to the Resident.
* **Resident cannot receive funds directly** — If no payments were received from the resident personally, there is no personal overpayment to refund.


---

## Procedure


1. Review the ledger to confirm the source of all rent payments
2. If 100% from housing authority → contact the agency
3. If agency confirms overpayment and sends written request → process refund check payable to the agency


---

## Email Template — Housing Assistance Fund Refund Request (to Agency)

> **Subject: Refund Request from Participant \[Tenant Name\] // \[Property Address\]**
>
> Hi \[Housing Authority / Caseworker Name\],
>
> I am writing to inform you that your participant, \[Tenant Name\], has contacted us requesting a refund for what they believe is a rent overpayment on their account.
>
> After reviewing the ledger, we confirmed the following:
>
> 100% of the rent payments received for this tenancy have come directly from your agency/program (\[Program Name\]). No payments have ever been received from the tenant personally.
>
> Our standard policy — and in most cases a requirement of the housing assistance program — that any refund of overpaid rent be returned exclusively to the original funding source (your agency). We are therefore unable to issue any refund directly to the tenant.
>
> If your agency has determined that an overpayment was made and you would like us to return those funds, please send us a written request specifying the exact amount and the months in question. We will process the refund check payable to your agency promptly upon receipt of that request.
>
> Please let us know if you need a copy of the tenant's ledger or any additional documentation to assist with your review.
>
> Thank you for your continued partnership.
>
> Thank you,

## Email Template — Housing Assistance Refund Denial (to Resident)

> **Subject: Re: Refund Request // \[Property Address\]**
>
> Hi \[Tenant Name\],
>
> Thank you for your recent email regarding a requested refund for an alleged overpayment of rent.
>
> After carefully reviewing your tenancy file and all payment records, I can confirm the following:
>
> No payments have ever been received directly from you personally. All rent payments applied to your account have been received exclusively from \[Housing Provider / Program Name\].
>
> As is standard practice (and in many cases required by the funding agreements with housing assistance programs), any refund of overpaid rent can only be issued to the original source of the funds — in this case, the housing provider that actually made the payments.
>
> Since no funds were ever received from you directly, there is no personal overpayment to refund to you.
>
> If the housing provider has inadvertently overpaid and is entitled to a reimbursement, we will of course process that refund directly to them upon their written request and verification.
>
> Please feel free to have your caseworker or the housing program contact us directly if they believe an overpayment has occurred on their end. We are happy to work with them to resolve any discrepancies.
>
> Thank you for your understanding. Should you have any further questions, please don't hesitate to reach out.
>
> Regards,
