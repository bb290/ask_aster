> **Situation:** Resident contacts us after attempting to make a partial rent payment and discovering that partial payments have been blocked due to eviction paperwork being filed.


---

## Key Points

* **Partial payments blocked** — Once the eviction process is initiated through LT Services (attorney's office), partial payments are no longer accepted
* **Two ways to stop eviction** — Pay balance in full, or submit a written request for early move-out and vacate by an agreed-upon date
* **Eviction filing schedule** — Filed on the 4th Tuesday of each month; once filed, cannot be cancelled based on a promise of future payment
* **Rental assistance** — Resident will receive a Pay or Vacate Notice from LT Services; list of rental assistance agencies available on [sagareus.com/late-rent](http://www.sagareus.com/late-rent)

## Response

> **Subject:** Update on Rent Payment Status
>
> Hi \[Resident Name\],
>
> We understand there may be some confusion around your online rent payment options. Please note that partial rent payments are no longer being accepted because the eviction process has already been initiated through LT Services, our attorney's office.
>
> **Eviction Policy**
> Evictions are filed on the 4th Tuesday of each month. Once filed, we cannot cancel or pause the eviction based on a promise of future payment. At this stage, there are only two ways to stop the eviction process:
> • **Pay the balance in full**
> • **Submit a written request for early move-out** and vacate the unit by an agreed-upon date.
>
> You will receive a formal **Pay or Vacate Notice** from LT Services. This document may help you qualify for emergency rental assistance. A list of rental assistance agencies we've worked with is available [on our website.](http://www.sagareus.com/late-rent)
>
> Regards,
