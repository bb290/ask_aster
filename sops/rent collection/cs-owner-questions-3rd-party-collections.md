> **Situation:** Owner asks questions about the 3rd party collections process after a Resident's account has been sent to Hunter Warfield.


---

## Key Points

* **Hunter Warfield negotiates directly** — they are authorized to reduce balance by up to 30% to recover funds
* **Credit reporting** — outstanding balance will lower the Resident's credit score and show on all credit reports until settled
* **Timeline varies** — some Residents pay immediately, some set up a payment plan, some take years to resolve
* **Fee structure** — Hunter Warfield keeps 40% of recovered funds; remaining 60% is released to Sagareus, then issued to property owner
* **No other options** — if Hunter Warfield cannot collect, there are no further recovery options available


---

## Owner Inquiry

* Did you send this account to collections?
* Can you educate me so I know what to expect with collections? How long is the process, do the collectors get a one-time payment or several installments, do I get periodic updates?
* I hear that it'll show on their records but don't know what that means exactly.
* What happens if Residents don't pay? What other option is there to recoup the remaining balance?
* If they manage to collect, how much do they keep for fees? The rest goes to Sagareus then to landlord, or to landlord directly?

## Email Template — Owner Collections Update

> Hi \[Owner Name\],
>
> Thank you for your question regarding the collections status for \[Property Address\].
>
> The former Resident's outstanding balance of \[Balance\] has been sent to our 3rd party collections agency, Hunter Warfield. Here's what to expect:
>
> **How it works:** Hunter Warfield negotiates directly with the former Resident to recover funds. They are authorized to reduce the balance by up to 30% if needed to reach a settlement.
>
> **Credit reporting:** The outstanding balance is reported to credit bureaus and will remain on the Resident's credit report until settled. This is a strong incentive for them to resolve the debt.
>
> **Timeline:** Some former Residents pay immediately, others set up a payment plan, and some cases take longer to resolve. There is no set timeline — Hunter Warfield will continue pursuing the balance.
>
> **Fees:** Hunter Warfield retains 40% of any recovered funds. The remaining 60% is released to Sagareus and then distributed to you.
>
> **If they don't pay:** Unfortunately, there are no additional recovery options beyond 3rd party collections.
>
> Please let me know if you have any other questions.
>
> Thank you,
