> **Situation:** Resident contacts us or we find a reversed payment with reason listed as "Account is Closed or Doesn't Match Name Submitted."


---

## Key Points

* **Typically an honest mistake** — usually a typo in account number or use of an old, closed account
* **Waive the fee** — delete the reversed payment fee from the ledger
* **Notify Resident** — send both a response email and a formal notification


---

## Procedure


1. Delete reversed payment fee from ledger
2. Send response email (if Resident contacted us)
3. Send notification email (proactive notice)

## Email Template — Reversed Payment Response

> The reason your payment was reversed is listed as: "Account is closed or doesn't match the name submitted."
>
> This usually means there may have been a typo when entering your account number, or the information submitted was linked to an old, closed account.
>
> Please double-check the account details and try submitting the payment again.
>
> As this appears to be an honest mistake, I've gone ahead and waived the returned check fee.
>
> Let me know if you need any help updating your payment information!
>
> Best,

## Email Template — Reversed Payment Notification

> **Subject: Action Needed: Returned Rent Payment Notification**
>
> Hi \[Resident Name\],
>
> We wanted to notify you that your recent rent payment was reversed. The reason provided was: "Account is closed or doesn't match the name submitted."
>
> This typically indicates a typo in the account number or the use of an old, closed account. Please log into the Resident Portal to double-check your payment details and resubmit your payment as soon as possible.
>
> As this appears to be an honest mistake, we've waived the returned check fee.
>
> If you need any assistance updating your information, feel free to reach out — we're happy to help.
>
> Best,
