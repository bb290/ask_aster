## Email Template — Notice to Vacate - End of Lease / Proper Notice Provided

> **Subject: Notice to Vacate - Proper Notice // \[Property Address\]**
>
> \[Owner Name\],
>
> Residents have submitted notice to vacate with a scheduled move-out date of \[Move Out Date\]. Unless we hear otherwise, Sagareus will proceed with sourcing a new Resident for the property. If you do not wish to re-rent at this time, please let us know as soon as possible.
>
> No further action is required from you right now. We will handle the Resident's move-out coordination, complete the move-out inspection, and deposit withholding. If we proceed with re-renting, you'll receive a separate email to review lease terms, pricing, and turn over recommendations prior to listing.
>
> Thank you,

## Email Template — Notice to Vacate - DV Situation

> **Subject: Notice to Vacate - Early Termination / DV Situation // \[Property Address\]**
>
> \[Owner Name\],
>
> We have received notice that the Resident intends to vacate early with a scheduled move-out date of \[Move Out Date\] due to circumstances covered under Washington State domestic violence protections ([RCW 59.18.575](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.18.575)).
>
> State law requires landlords to allow early termination of the lease without penalty in these situations, and the security deposit must be refunded in full. We are in the process of collecting the necessary documentation from the Resident to substantiate their claim and to prepare for a mitigation fund application with the state.
>
> Unless we hear otherwise, Sagareus will proceed with sourcing a new Resident for the property. If you do not wish to re-rent at this time, please let us know as soon as possible.
>

If we proceed with re-renting, you'll receive a separate email to review lease terms, pricing, and turn over recommendations prior to listing.
