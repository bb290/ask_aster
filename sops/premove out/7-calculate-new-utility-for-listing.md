## Determine Pass Through Rules for All Utilities

**IF separate meter is available for a unit:**

* **Water / Sewer** — Paid to management, passed through to ledger. Cost based on actual usage; historical average \~$80/mo. Supported by Estimated Utility Cost Report.
* **Garbage** — Paid by resident directly to utility whenever possible. If not: paid to management, passed through to ledger. Cost based on actual usage; historical average \~$80/mo (2-adult household). Supported by Estimated Utility Cost Report.
* **Electricity / Gas** — Paid directly to Puget Sound Energy. Historical \~$65/mo (2-adult household). Supported by Estimated Utility Cost Report.

**IF shared meter:**

* Flat fee — see the Flat Fee calculation below.
* Run a new analysis each Lease Up; utility costs rise over time.
* Upload analysis to the LU task for leasing team reference. Owners and residents ask for this frequently.

## How to Calculate Flat Fee Utility / Utility Amount


:::info
Flat Fee Utility is calculated **by percentage of bedrooms per unit** multiplied by the **last 12 months actual utility cost**.

In the past, Sagareus charged flat fee utilities per person; this proved inaccurate — 2 people do not use twice as much utility.

:::


1. Prepare and download the Flat Fee Cost Analysis Report for each listing.
2. Upload to:

   
   1. This subtask
   2. LU Task (for Leasing Team)
3. **IF utility data is missing / unavailable:**

   
   1. Estimate Flat Fee based on the closest property under management with the same bedroom count.
   2. Make a note in both Asana tasks: `Utilities estimated, missing data`.
4. Use the [Flat Fee Utility Cost Calculator](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1rWLTFUl8htA2_pxsXOZ-X0WOELmX7-Va_Eze5WnX0vU/edit?usp=sharing):

   
   1. Duplicate the Master Calculator Tab.

* [Gas Flat Fee Utility Calculator](https://www.loom.com/share/0ec540edb566450d8f24ea1e36af970a?sid=db09601a-4a34-4797-b8db-57eeaab9a392)
* [W/S/G Flat Fee Utility Calculator](https://www.loom.com/share/c9a2d75dc8764af79c9b5d3525a92c2d?sid=41a61bda-b8f7-4cc5-9e22-0ce55d8afdef)

**Calculation rules:**


1. Calculate each utility separately (Water/Sewer, Garbage, Electricity).
   * Include at least half a year of service for Electricity to capture seasonal variance.
   * Use at least two Water/Sewer bills for an average.
   * Reflect the accurate count of OCCUPIED units for multifamily.
2. Input data to each green box in the calculator:
   * Property Address
   * Date range of utility expense reporting (at least 6 months for electricity; 2+ bills for W/S/G)
   * Total utility expense by line item — Buildium → Reports → Income Statement; Custom date, From: 12 months ago, To: Today; Interval: Year. Scroll to bottom under Expense Line: UTILITIES. Only include utilities in the flat fee / not submetered.
3. Input the # of occupied bedrooms for the entire building (Buildium → Reports → Rent Roll).
4. Create a report for each utility we receive bills for.
5. Prepare the report for download — remove the word "Master" from the tab name, double-check formatting and math.
6. Download: File → Download → PDF. Current Sheet, Portrait, Fit to Height, center horizontally and vertically, no gridlines. Headers & Footers: Workbook Title, Sheet Name, Current Date.
7. Rename the file: `<Address> - Sagareus Accounting - <Report Name>`.

## How to Calculate Estimated Utility Cost


:::info
Estimated Utility Cost is provided to prospective residents during Lease Up. Preparing this proactively equips the team to answer a commonly asked question and lease up faster.

:::

* Prepare and download Estimated Utility Cost Report for each listing; upload to this subtask and to the LU task.
* If utility data is missing/unavailable from the Income Statement: make a note in both Asana tasks — `Utilities data unavailable`.
* Use the [Estimated Pass Thru Utility Cost](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1rWLTFUl8htA2_pxsXOZ-X0WOELmX7-Va_Eze5WnX0vU/edit?usp=sharing) calculator. Duplicate the Master Calculator Tab.
* Input: Property Address; past 6 months of actual utility costs (only pass-through utilities); delete unnecessary rows.
