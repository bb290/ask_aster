> **Situation:** A resident objects to the annual inspection. They may question whether the visit has to happen, raise privacy concerns about photos, or be reluctant to schedule.


---

## Key Points

* **Annual inspection is mandatory.** Every property under management is inspected once per year. We cannot waive at resident request.
* **WA law gives us the right.** RCW 59.18.150(1) provides that a tenant *"shall not unreasonably withhold consent"* to landlord entry for inspection.
* **Lead with collaboration.** The goal of this outreach is to work with the resident on a time that fits their schedule.
* **Photo policy reassurance.** Most pushback is privacy-driven. Walk through the narrow photo scope and emphasize that personal items are never photographed.
* **Document everything.** Every outreach, objection, and confirmation goes in the Asana task as a comment.
* **If scheduling fails, separate process.** After 5 total outreaches with no resolution, reassign to Ops Manager.


---

## Resident Inquiry — General Scheduling Pushback

### Email Template — Scheduling Pushback

> **Subject:** Annual Inspection // [Property Address]
>
> Hi [Resident Name],
>
> Thanks for sharing your concerns. Annual inspections are part of how we manage every property, and we'd like to find a time that fits your week.
>
> For context, RCW 59.18.150 establishes that Sagareus has the right to enter for the annual inspection and that residents may not unreasonably withhold consent.
>
> **Why we do it.** Once a year, we confirm life-safety items are working and document the condition of the home and exterior. The visit takes 15 to 20 minutes.
>
> **What we look at**
> - Smoke and carbon monoxide detectors
> - Plumbing under sinks (checking for active leaks)
> - Appliances
> - Roof, gutters, and landscape (from outside)
>
> **Photo policy.** Photos are limited to the items above. We do not photograph personal belongings, and photos are never used for marketing.
>
> Could you share a few times that work for you in the next two weeks?
>
> Thank you,

## Resident Inquiry — Photo Concerns

### Email Template — Photo Concerns Response

> **Subject:** Annual Inspection Photography Policy // [Property Address]
>
> Hi [Resident Name],
>
> Thank you for sharing your concerns. We take resident privacy very seriously and want to make the inspection as respectful and minimally invasive as possible.
>
> Annual Inspections are conducted just once per year to identify maintenance needs, ensure lease compliance, and protect the property's long-term condition.
>
> **During the inspection, staff will only photograph required items**, including:
>
> - The presence and condition of smoke and carbon monoxide detectors
> - The area under sinks to confirm no active leaks
> - The condition and inventory of appliances
> - Any clear maintenance concerns or lease violations
> - Exterior areas like the roof, gutters, landscaping
>
> Photos are strictly for internal documentation purposes and are never used for marketing.
>
> Thank you,


---

## Procedure — If Resident Continues to Refuse


1. Send one more written exchange re-offering flexible scheduling.
2. If still refused or unresponsive after **5 total outreaches**, reassign the Asana task to the Ops Manager.
3. Ops Manager handles the formal notice-of-entry path.
4. Document the entry attempt and any further refusal.
