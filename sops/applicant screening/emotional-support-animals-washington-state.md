:::warning
Under the federal Fair Housing Act and Washington's Law Against Discrimination (RCW 49.60), an emotional support animal is a disability accommodation, not a pet. When a Resident provides valid documentation from a licensed professional, Sagareus is legally required to allow the animal to live in the home, even at a property with a no-pet policy. This obligation applies regardless of an Owner's personal preference regarding pets, and it exists to protect both the Owner and Sagareus from fair housing liability.
:::

This policy explains how Sagareus handles emotional support animals (ESAs) at the properties we manage, and the legal framework that requires it. It exists to:

* Keep properties compliant with fair housing law.
* Protect Owners from liability.
* Set clear expectations so there are no surprises.

---

## Legal Basis

ESA housing protections come from two layers of law:

* **Federal Fair Housing Act** requires housing providers to make reasonable accommodations for Residents with disabilities who need an assistance animal.
* **Washington Law Against Discrimination (RCW 49.60)** reinforces and, in some respects, strengthens those protections at the state level, prohibiting housing discrimination based on disability and the use of assistance animals. Washington is regarded as a strongly tenant-protective state on this issue.

:::info
**2026 federal guidance change.** In 2026, federal enforcement guidance around ESAs shifted. Washington State law independently protects ESAs in housing, so this federal change does not reduce the obligation to accommodate a properly documented ESA. Sagareus continues to follow Washington law.
:::

---

## What This Means for the Property

* **No pet fees for an ESA.** Because an ESA is legally an accommodation rather than a pet, Sagareus cannot charge pet rent, a pet deposit, or any pet-related surcharge for it. The Resident remains fully responsible for any damage the animal causes beyond normal wear and tear, and that responsibility is enforced through their security deposit at move-out, just as it would be for any other damage.
* **No breed, weight, or species restrictions.** Standard pet restrictions do not apply to an approved ESA, and an ESA is not limited to dogs and cats.
* **No-pet properties still apply.** If a property is marketed as no-pets, that policy remains in place for ordinary pets, but a valid ESA must still be accommodated as a matter of law.
* **Documentation is required.** Sagareus does not simply take a Resident's word for it. Accommodation depends on legitimate documentation from a licensed healthcare professional. Washington does not recognize online "certificates," "registrations," ID cards, or vests as proof, and neither does Sagareus. The professional documentation is verified before any ESA is approved.
* **Housing only.** These protections apply to housing. They do not extend to public spaces or air travel.

---

## Damage Protection

The biggest concern for most Owners is property damage, and an ESA does not leave the Owner exposed. Sagareus documents the condition of the home with a detailed inspection and photos at move-in, the Resident is financially responsible for any damage beyond normal wear and tear, and that liability is enforced against their deposit at move-out. An ESA changes what can be charged up front. It does not change the Resident's obligation to return the property in good condition.

---

## Limited Exceptions

The law allows an accommodation to be denied only in narrow, fact-specific circumstances, for example where a specific animal poses a genuine, direct threat to the health or safety of others, or would cause significant property damage, and where that risk cannot be reduced through reasonable accommodation. These are high legal standards. When a situation like this arises, Sagareus evaluates it carefully and, where appropriate, with legal guidance, before taking any action, so that both the Owner and Sagareus remain protected.
