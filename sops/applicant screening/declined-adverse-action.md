:::warning
This policy ensures compliance with the **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)** and other applicable laws when an applicant is declined for tenancy, employment, or credit based on a consumer report or other information. The procedure outlines the steps for providing timely and appropriate notice of adverse action.

:::

When an applicant is declined based on information obtained from a consumer report or other relevant sources, the company must provide an adverse action notice. This notice informs the applicant of the decision, the reason for the denial, and their rights under the law.


---

## Adverse Action Notice


:::info
Notice must include:

* A statement that the applicant has been declined.
* The specific reason(s) for the denial (e.g., credit history, rental history, criminal background, etc.).
* Contact information for the consumer reporting agency used.
* A statement that the agency did not make the decision and cannot provide reasons for the action.
* A notice of the applicant's right to obtain a free copy of their report within 60 days.
* A notice of their right to dispute the accuracy of the information in the report.

:::
