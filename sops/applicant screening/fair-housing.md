## Sagareus Fair Housing Policy


:::warning
Sagareus Real Estate does not discriminate based on Race, Color, Creed, National Origin, Sex, Sexual Orientation, Gender Identity, Disability, Marital Status, HIV/AIDS or Hepatitis C Status, Families with children, Use of Dog Guide or Service Animal, Honorably Discharged Veteran or Military Status, Retaliation, Immigration or Citizenship status.

:::

* We gladly receive inquiries from all and apply fair and equitable criteria when evaluating applicants. We enforce our rules equally and without discrimination.
* We set rents, deposits, and fees without discrimination.
* We respond to repair requests and other resident concerns equally.
* We provide reasonable accommodations for people with disabilities.


:::info
If you feel you were discriminated against at any point in your interaction with Sagareus Real Estate, please complete the form to the right.

All claims will be handled with discretion and addressed immediately.

:::

## Fair Housing Laws Overview


:::warning
All Sagareus Staff are expected to work in compliance with the Fair Housing Act (FHA), the Washington Law Against Discrimination (WLAD), and local fair housing laws.

:::


:::warning
All staff must comply with:

* **Federal Fair Housing Act (FHA)** – Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation), familial status, and disability.
* **Washington Law Against Discrimination (WLAD)** – Adds protections for marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, veteran/military status, and the use of service animals.
* **Seattle & Other Local Ordinances** – Additional protections may apply, such as source of income (SOI) protections and criminal background screening restrictions.

:::


---

### Prohibited Discriminatory Practices


:::warning
Staff **must not**:

* Refuse to rent or negotiate based on protected characteristics.
* Offer different lease terms, rental rates, or services.
* Provide false or misleading information about availability.
* Advertise with language that suggests preferences or limitations.
* Require different application requirements for different applicants.
* Engage in steering (directing Residents to certain units based on protected status).

:::


---

## Leasing Process Compliance

### Advertising & Marketing


:::warning
* Use inclusive and neutral language in listings.
* Avoid discriminatory terms like "perfect for young professionals" or "ideal for couples."

:::


---

### Screening & Application Process


:::warning
* Use **consistent** application criteria for all applicants.
* **Source of Income Protection**: Cannot deny applicants based on legal sources of income (e.g., Section 8 vouchers, Social Security, child support).
* Follow **criminal background screening laws** (Seattle's "Fair Chance Housing" law limits how criminal records can be used in screening).

:::


---

### Lease Agreements & Resident Interactions


:::warning
* Offer **identical lease terms** to all approved applicants.
* Make reasonable accommodations for persons with disabilities (e.g., allowing service animals despite a no-pets policy).
* Maintain **professional, neutral communication** at all times, in all forms.

:::


---

### Handling Complaints & Violations


:::warning
Document and **report all complaints** related to potential discrimination to management immediately via [www.sagareus.com/fair-housing](http://www.sagareus.com/fair-housing).

:::
