Standard responses to the lease questions applicants ask most, in Sagareus voice. Reply on the original email thread.

## Under What Circumstances Would a Supplementary Credit Report Be Run?

> Hi [Resident's Name],
>
> Great question — we include that clause in the lease primarily as a safeguard. In practice, we rarely run supplementary credit reports during a tenancy. However, it may be considered in limited circumstances such as:
>
> **If a Resident requests to add a new leaseholder or roommate**, and we need to screen them for qualification; **If the lease is being renewed and there's a history of late or missed payments**, we may review updated credit to assess financial risk; **If legal or collection proceedings become necessary**, a report may be used to determine next steps.
>
> Outside of those scenarios, we do not routinely request credit reports after move-in.
>
> Please let me know if you'd like to discuss further or have additional questions.

## Can You Reduce or Remove the Early Termination Fee?

> Hi [Resident's Name],
>
> Our standard lease includes a flat early termination fee equal to two months' rent, along with rent responsibility through the re-rental date. This structure is in place to provide clarity and fairness across all lease breaks.
>
> The early termination fee remains in place to offset turnover and leasing costs, currently budgeted to occur no more than once per year.

## Can the Lease Convert to Month-to-Month After the Initial 12 Months?

> Hi [Resident's Name],
>
> We do not offer month-to-month leases due to rental regulation restrictions, which make such arrangements difficult to manage for both Residents and property owners.
>
> However, we're happy to offer a shorter-term fixed lease (such as 3 or 6 months) after the initial 12-month term to support flexibility around your next move.
>
> The only stipulation is that we do not end leases in November, December, or January, as those are the most challenging months for re-renting and often result in extended vacancy for property owners.

## Can Rent Be Increased During the Lease Term?

Example applicant question: "I noticed the last month's rent prepayment note mentions the possibility of a rent increase before the last month of tenancy. What circumstances might cause that? Could rent change during the current lease period?"

> Hi [Resident's Name],
>
> Rent can only be increased at the end of the current lease term with proper notice. Different cities have different notice requirements, below is a breakdown of the notice dates:
>
> **Rent Increase (Seattle)** — notices served 180 days prior to rent increase.
>
> **Rent Increase (Tacoma)** — notices served 180 / 120 / 90 days prior to rent increase.
>
> **All other jurisdictions in WA** — WA requires 90 days notice. HOWEVER, since several local jurisdictions require 120 days when the increase exceeds 5%, **Sagareus provides rent increase notice for ALL non-Seattle/non-Tacoma jurisdictions at 120 days**.
>
> **Renewal** — the renewal offer must be served to the Resident **between** 60-90 days before the end of the lease. Serving earlier than 90 days will NOT meet the WA requirement (confirmed by attorney).

## Can the Deposit and Last Month's Rent Apply to the Early Termination Fee?

Example applicant question: "Would the deposit and last month of rent be used for the fee of 2x rent in case of early termination?"

> Hi [Resident's Name],
>
> Great question! In the case of early termination, the termination fee is equal to two times the monthly rent and is due at the time notice is given.
>
> The security deposit is not applied upfront, but after move-out, it can be used toward the early termination fee — after any applicable deductions for damages, cleaning, or unpaid utility charges have been assessed. Any remaining deposit balance would then be applied to the fee.
>
> The last month's rent will still be applied to your final month of occupancy, assuming proper notice is given.
>
> Let me know if you have any other questions!
