> **Situation:** A third party (e.g., new landlord, employer) requests verification of a Resident's tenancy.


---

## Key Points

* **Signed authorization required** — Must have signed authorization from the Resident before releasing any information
* **Use our template only** — Do NOT use the 3rd party's provided form
* **Limited information** — Only confirm tenancy status, lease dates, and monthly rent amount
* **Rent history omitted** — To account for OLD (Order of Limited Dissemination) and avoid Fair Housing violation accusations
* **Applied uniformly** — Same response for all Residents regardless of standing


---


:::warning
Use the templated response below, DO NOT use the 3rd party's provided form.

* This list is intentionally short and leaves out rent history information or other subjective information.
  * Rent history is omitted to account for OLD (Order of Limited Dissemination)
  * Avoids accusation of Fair Housing Violation
* This rule is **applied uniformly for all Residents**, regardless of whether the Resident is in good standing or behind on rent.

:::

## Procedure


1. Verify if the 3rd party provided **Signed authorization from the Resident** to release information
   * If NOT — notify 3rd party we cannot disclose information without signed authorization
2. Look up existing renewal / rent increase task and update to indicate 3rd party Resident verification received
