Once applicants are approved, the Leasing Assistant drafts the lease for e-signature. This is the hub for everything between approval and a signed lease: drafting defaults, addenda, negotiation scenarios, and standard answers to applicant questions.

Video walkthrough: https://www.loom.com/share/294d6ce4cf3b4a049ce9e170f6d98cbe

:::warning
Three legal rules shape this process. Leases over 12 months require a notary (RCW 59.18.210). Payment plans for move in costs must always be accepted; it is the law. Security deposits and last month's rent are tenant-held trust funds; rent credits can never be applied to them.
:::

## Policies

:::warning
- Draft from the current PandaDoc template with the default terms unless the Asana task says otherwise.
- The listing stays live and showings continue until the lease is signed. Check for signatures twice a day and follow up daily.
- No signature and no response after 48 hours means the applicant is assumed no longer interested: cancel the lease.
- Tacoma leases cannot include last month rent.
:::

## Workflow

:::info
Confirm terms, draft from the template, attach the right addenda, send for e-signature, then chase signatures until the lease is executed.
:::

1. Confirm lease terms from the most recent listing and LU Task notes. De-listed and unclear? Send the Lease Term Confirmation email before drafting: [Lease Negotiation // Lease Term Over 12 Months](lease-negotiation-lease-term-over-12-months.md) covers the verification steps.
2. Draft from the template with the [default terms and dates](lease-drafting-default-terms-dates-prorated-rent.md).
3. Work through the addenda: [landscaping checkbox, pet, DADU](lease-drafting-landscaping-pet-dadu-addenda.md), [utilities](lease-drafting-utility-addendum.md), and the [General Addendum](lease-drafting-general-addendum.md) for anything property-specific.
4. Send for e-signature.
5. Follow up: check for signatures twice a day, follow up daily, and cancel at 48 hours of silence per the policy above.
