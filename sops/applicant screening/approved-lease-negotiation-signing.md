### Applicant Requests Lease over 12 months


:::warning
Per Washington State law (RCW 59.18.210), any residential lease that exceeds one year must be signed in the presence of a notary. [RCW](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=59.18.210)

:::


1. An applicant requests a lease term longer than 12 months.
2. This is allowed but is subject to approval by Leasing Manager
3. Per Washington State law (RCW 59.18.210), any residential lease that exceeds one year must be signed in the presence of a notary.

### Calculating Prorated Rent

* **Determine the monthly rent** – the full rent amount for the month including rent, utility, pet, parking and any additional monthly fee.
  * (Total monthly cost x 12) / 365 = daily rate
  * Daily rate x number of days = Prorated Rent

If an applicant asks if we will accept a payment plan for move in costs, the answer is always **YES**. This is required by law.

   2. Payment 1 is due on the following 1st of the month

   * Split the monthly payment into 2 payments – One toward security deposit; One toward Last month rent

### Move In Bonus Rent Credit to Move In Costs / Incentive


:::warning
**Move In Bonus = Rent Credit.** Rent credits may only be applied toward rent. They cannot be applied to the security deposit or last month's rent deposit.

:::

**Reasoning:**

Both the security deposit and last month's rent deposit are considered tenant-held trust funds under Washington State law. These funds must be held in a separate trust account and remain the legal property of the tenant until properly disbursed at move-out.

Rent credits, by contrast, represent a temporary rent reduction, not a transfer of trust funds, and therefore cannot legally or operationally be applied to deposits.

#### Pass Thru Utility Billing


:::info
Pass thru means the actual cost will be added to the Residents ledger as received.

:::

#### Flat Fee Utility Billing


:::info
Flat fee means that Residents will be charged the same amount every month regardless of the actual bill cost.

:::

#### General Addendum


:::info
The purpose of the **General Addendum** is to provide a controlled, standardized method for documenting **property-specific, non-standard, or situational lease terms** that do not warrant a standalone addendum but must be disclosed and acknowledged in writing.

:::
