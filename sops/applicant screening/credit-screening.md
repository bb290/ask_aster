## Credit Screening Policies + Procedures

To qualify for a Sagareus-managed home, applicants must demonstrate a credit history that reflects financial responsibility, reliability in meeting obligations, and a low risk of default.


:::info
The following Policies and Procedures outline how Sagareus evaluates credit, how the final credit score is interpreted, and how approval decisions are made.

:::

## Credit Requirement Tiers

**Lenient Tier: Minimum 600 Credit Score**

* Lower-income or workforce housing
* Slower-leasing areas
* Units where the property owner prefers a broader applicant pool

**Standard Tier: Minimum 650 Credit Score (Default)**

* Most Sagareus properties across the portfolio
* Properties where owners expect stable tenant profiles

**Stringent Tier: Minimum 700 Credit Score**

* High-end rentals or premium neighborhoods
* Properties with HOA or condo rules requiring higher tenant screening standards


---

### How the Household Credit Score Is Determined


:::info
Sagareus uses the median credit score of all adult applicants to determine whether the household meets the required tier for the property.

:::

* Each adult applicant (18+) undergoes an individual credit check
* All individual credit scores are collected and arranged in numerical order.
* The household's "Final Credit Score" is the *median* of those scores.

#### Example 1: Two Applicants

* Applicant A: 710
* Applicant B: 640
* Median = (710 + 640) ÷ 2 = 675

#### Example 2: Three Applicants

* Applicant A: 605, B: 672, C: 720
* Ordered: 605, 672, 720 → Median = 672

## Co-Signers


:::warning
A co-signer may be permitted when the household's final median credit score is within 50 points of the required tier minimum.

:::

### When a Co-Signer Is Allowed

* The household score is close to qualifying but does not meet the minimum by 50 points or less, AND
* No automatic denial criteria are present, AND
* Income requirements are fully met.


---

## Automatic Denial Criteria


:::warning
The following credit-related findings result in automatic denial, regardless of score or tier.

:::

### Credit Score Is More Than 50 Points Below Minimum

If the household's final median credit score is more than 50 points below the required tier minimum, the application is automatically denied.

### Funds Owed to a Previous Landlord

Any outstanding balance owed to a prior landlord is an automatic denial until fully resolved.

### Eviction on Record

Any eviction filing or judgment within the last 7 years is an automatic denial.

### Open Bankruptcy

* Open (active) Chapter 7 or 13 bankruptcy = automatic denial
* Discharged bankruptcies may be considered if credit has re-established stability

### Fraud Indicators

* Suspected or confirmed identity fraud
* Forged or altered documentation
* Mismatched personal information
* Inability to verify identity
