:::warning
Per the executed Property Management Agreement, Sagareus is authorized "to advertise and promote the Property for rent, and to negotiate and execute leases to rent the Property to qualified Residents." Lease negotiation and execution authority rests with Sagareus, not the Owner. Inserting Owner approval into applicant selection or lease offers creates Fair Housing and First-In-Time exposure for both Sagareus and the Owner.
:::

This policy exists to:

* Keep leasing operations efficient, minimize vacancy, and maintain alignment with market conditions.
* Ensure Fair Housing laws are upheld.
* Protect Sagareus and property Owners from potential liability.

---

## Authority to Negotiate

* **Sagareus retains the right to negotiate lease terms** — including pricing adjustments, incentives, and other concessions — to secure qualified Residents. This authority is essential to act swiftly in a dynamic rental market.
* **Owners set their applicant criteria at onboarding** — Sagareus enforces these criteria fairly and identically across all applicants. See [Mgr Review](/doc/ekhiZgRBbG) and [Credit Screening](/doc/S8butaoYeI). The Owner's input happens when criteria are set at onboarding; there is no per-application Owner approval step.
* **First-In-Time** — inside Seattle, the unit must be offered to the first qualified applicant who submits a complete application. Holding an offer for Owner review conflicts with this law. See [Section 8 // First Come, First Served (First-In-Time Rule)](/doc/ynzTXsaFqH).

---

## Pricing Strategy

* **Owners may provide pricing preferences or minimum rent thresholds.**
* **Sagareus determines final pricing strategy** based on market conditions and Resident demand.
* **Adjustments remain within reason** and align with the Owner's long-term objectives.
* Owners stay informed through the [Email | Owner - Weekly Activity Report](/doc/VDE6GnaYef).

---

## Rent Control Considerations

* Sagareus monitors legislative changes, including potential rent control developments in Washington State.
* Strategies will be adjusted as needed to ensure compliance and protect Owner interests.
* Until specific regulations are enacted, the priority remains securing Residents quickly and competitively.

---

## Dispute Resolution

* **If a conflict arises over pricing decisions** — Sagareus will review the concerns and may, at its discretion, pause lease negotiations to align on expectations.
* **Repeated delays or restrictions** that hinder the ability to lease the property effectively may result in a request to amend or terminate the management agreement.

## Response Template — Owner Requests to Approve Applicants or Lease Terms

Reply to the original email thread.

:::info
Hi [Owner Name],

Thank you for reaching out. We completely understand wanting to stay close to decisions about your property.

Under our Property Management Agreement, Sagareus is authorized to negotiate and execute leases with qualified Residents on your behalf. Every applicant is evaluated against the screening criteria established for your property at onboarding, applied identically to all applicants. Keeping approvals inside that process is what protects you: Fair Housing law requires consistent criteria for every applicant, and pausing an offer for case-by-case review creates legal exposure for both of us and risks losing qualified applicants in a fast-moving market.

[Remove if the property is not in Seattle] **Seattle's First-In-Time rule also legally requires us to offer the unit to the first qualified applicant who submits a complete application, so we are not able to hold offers for separate review.**

You are always welcome to share pricing preferences or a minimum rent threshold, and we factor those into our strategy along with current market conditions. We will keep you updated on all activity through your weekly report.

:::
