> **Situation:** An owner asks whether Sagareus can decline a Section 8 applicant, or requests that their property not be rented to Section 8 voucher holders.


---

## Key Points

* **It is illegal to deny** — Washington State law ([RCW 59.18.255](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.18.255)) prohibits landlords from refusing to rent based on a tenant's lawful source of income, which includes Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers. This is also protected under the Washington Law Against Discrimination ([RCW 49.60.222](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=49.60.222)).
* **Sagareus always accepts Section 8** — As a matter of both legal compliance and company policy, Sagareus does not decline applicants based on their use of a housing voucher. This is non-negotiable.
* **Same screening criteria apply** — Section 8 applicants go through the exact same screening process as all applicants (credit, background, rental history, income verification). The voucher amount counts toward the income requirement.
* **Seattle has additional protections** — Seattle's Fair Housing Ordinance ([SMC 14.08](https://library.municode.com/wa/seattle/codes/municipal_code?nodeId=TIT14_HURI_CH14.08_UNFAM)) and King County's Fair Housing Ordinance ([KCC 12.20](https://kingcounty.gov/en/legacy/council/legislation/kc-code/24-title-12)) provide additional source-of-income protections with active enforcement.
* **Penalties for violations** — Discrimination based on source of income can result in complaints to the Washington State Human Rights Commission, civil lawsuits, damages, and attorney fees.
* **Education, not confrontation** — Many owners are unaware that source-of-income discrimination is illegal in Washington. Our role is to educate them professionally and reassure them about the process.

Reply to the original email thread.

> Hi [Owner Name],
>
> Thank you for reaching out. I understand your question regarding Section 8 applicants.
>
> Under Washington State law (RCW 59.18.255), source of income — including Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) — is a protected class. This means we are legally required to consider all applicants who use a housing voucher, just as we would any other applicant.
>
> That said, Section 8 applicants go through the exact same screening process as every other applicant. We verify credit, rental history, background, and income using the same criteria. The housing voucher simply counts toward the income requirement — it does not bypass any part of our screening.
>
> Additionally, the property will be inspected by the housing authority, which helps ensure the home is maintained to a high standard. The housing authority also guarantees a portion of the rent directly to us, which provides reliable income for your property.
>
> We're happy to walk you through how the process works in more detail. Please let us know if you have any other questions.
