:::warning
Once the RFTA is approved by the housing authority, they will schedule an inspection. **Respond immediately** — any delay on our end can add another week or more to the already lengthy Section 8 process.

:::

## Scheduling the Inspection


1. Once the RFTA is approved, the housing authority will contact Sagareus to schedule an inspection
2. **Respond immediately** to the inspector / case manager with access details and let them know they can access anytime
3. Coordinate with the tenant (if applicable) and the turnover team to ensure the property is accessible


---

## Common Inspection Failure Items


:::warning
If the property is likely to fail on any of the items below, **notify the tenant early** before the inspection is scheduled. Failing an initial inspection adds significant delay — the inspector must return for a re-inspection after repairs are completed.

:::

Housing authority inspections follow **Housing Quality Standards (HQS)**.

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **GFCI Outlets** | Working GFCI-protected outlets in **kitchen, bathroom, laundry room, and garage** |
| **Windows** | All windows must be modern, functional, and open/close properly. |
| **Paint (Pre-1978 homes)** | **No flaking, peeling, or chipping paint** anywhere in the home if built prior to 1978 (lead paint concern). |
| **Handrails** | Handrails required for **all stairs** — including exterior stairs, porch steps, and yard stairs. |
| **Smoke & CO Detectors** | Working smoke detectors in every bedroom and on every level. CO detectors where required. |
| **Plumbing** | No leaks, all fixtures functional, hot water available |
| **Electrical** | All outlets and switches functional, proper cover plates |
| **Security** | Working locks on all exterior doors and windows |


---

## After the Inspection


1. Inspector will almost always respond with a list of repairs needed
2. Upload the inspection report to the Section 8 subtask in Asana
3. **Follow up with the inspector** until the full list of repairs needed is received
4. **Assign repairs to the Turnover Coordinator**
5. Once repairs are completed, notify the housing authority to schedule a **re-inspection**
