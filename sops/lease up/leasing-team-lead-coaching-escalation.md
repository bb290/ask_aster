How Team Leads keep the team from drifting: the coaching loop, worked examples, and what escalates every time.

:::info
**Entropy**: things fall apart unless someone actively holds them together. Without structure, standards, and enforcement, any team drifts toward disorder.
:::

## The Coaching Loop: Observe, Diagnose, Prescribe, Practice, Follow-Up

1. **Observe** — questions agents ask; spot-check calendar, listings, inspection reports, interactions, Asana tasks and due dates.
2. **Diagnose** — document where SOPs are misapplied or misinterpreted. Pick 1-2 to focus on at a time.
3. **Prescribe + feedback** — "Kind not nice": honest, kind feedback; holding back is more hurtful because consistent underperformance ends in termination. Always link the actual SOP not being executed to standard.
4. **Practice** — assign a specific SOP focus for the coming week.
5. **Follow-Up** — evaluate at the next 1:1. Repeat up to 3 weeks; still lacking in week 4 means escalate.

## Worked Examples

- Low inquiry-to-showing conversion (10+ inquiries, 5 or fewer showings) → retrain on Respond to Inquiries / Speed to Lead SOP.
- Weekly Activity Report late → Calendar Blocking / Time Management training.
- Broke policy (keys before move-in funds) → retrain Move In Policies, emphasize the WHY, subtask to the Leasing Manager.
- Listing 30+ days on market → review Weekly Activity Report recommendations, thresholds and low-activity checklist, photo quality and order.

## Escalating Performance Issues

Create an Asana task with the violation and documentation, assign to the Leasing Manager, due today.

:::warning
**Poor performance, escalate every time:** recurring documented issue persisting 3 weeks; Weekly Activity Report after Tuesday 12pm; Move Out Report after 48 hours with no documented explanation; no call / no show; conduct or dress code violation.
:::

:::tip
**Positive performance, escalate every time (praise in public):** kind words from anyone; exceptional work on a listing, inspection, or report; going above and beyond.
:::
