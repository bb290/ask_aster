Non-negotiable rules governing how Sagareus conducts leasing activity.

## Showing Policy

A member of Sagareus Leasing Staff must accompany every property showing. Prospective Residents and third parties are never permitted to tour properties unattended.

This protects:

* Property security
* Consistency of information given to prospects
* A professional showing experience


---

## Pre-Leasing (Occupied Units)

Properties may be listed **2–4 weeks prior to Move Out**, but no more than 4 weeks early.

**Requirements:**

* Listing photos must be available.
* Use the availability date feature in Buildium — do not show the unit as available before it is.
* Engage early applicants using the **Pre-Leasing Form**.
  * Applicants who miss out on a property complete the [PreLeasing Form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSf6-QigCkwbvNmid5UzCoFnvoZEYcXy9e8DIrZdBrWRBHBQ0w/viewform).
  * [Leasing Agent access to pre-leasing leads](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1bQMu8RLP_NOKrHakx0F9_47ELtOk2FLg19A_TMuAs-E/edit?usp=sharing)

## Occupied Showings

Occupied showings are allowed only when **both** conditions are met:

* The property is well-maintained.
* The current Resident is cooperative.

**Before proposing a showing:**


1. Check the most recent Annual Inspection Report.
2. Review completed maintenance tasks — these may include interior photos.

**If the Resident declines:**


1. Respond politely and respect their decision.
2. After a few days, follow up:

   > *"Hi [Resident Name], would you feel comfortable with just me stopping by to capture a quick walk-through video? I've had a lot of interest and am scheduling showings for after move-out, but a video would really help me respond to people in the meantime."*
   * If **yes** — schedule a time. Capture a clean, respectful walk-through video to share with prospects.
   * If **no** — acknowledge politely. Let them know showings will begin after move-out. Use this moment to coordinate move-out tasks if relevant.

## Sandbag Approach (When Occupied Showings Are Not Possible)

When the Resident declines or the unit is not in showing condition, use the Sandbag Approach: strategically delay showings while managing prospect interest.

**Sandbag is appropriate when:**

* The home is cluttered or dirty
* The Resident is hostile or uncooperative
* The unit is inaccessible or disrupted by move prep

**How to sandbag effectively:**

* Schedule showings to begin **1–2 days after Move-Out** — allows time to inspect and adjust if condition is worse than expected.
* **Batch showings** — stack multiple prospects into the same time block.
* Communicate transparently with prospects.


:::tip
**Example Response to Prospective Resident**

*"Thanks so much for your interest! The current Residents have requested that I wait until after they move out to begin showings; our company policy is to respect Resident privacy as much as possible.*

*They're scheduled to vacate on July 31st, so we've set the first showing for August 1st. Would 2pm work for you, or is 6pm better?*

*Please note: the home may not be fully move-in ready at the time of your showing — minor repairs and professional cleaning may still be in progress. You're absolutely welcome to view the space to get a feel for the layout and features."*

:::

## Facebook Marketplace


:::warning
**Effective March 2026:** Sagareus does not advertise rental listings on Facebook Marketplace as part of its standard marketing strategy.

:::

This policy was implemented after a 12-month testing period. Key findings:


1. **Policy violations** — listings were flagged for commercial activity; the platform is not designed for professional property managers.
2. **Account limitations** — Facebook Marketplace requires personal accounts. Sagareus does not require employees to use personal social media for business operations.
3. **Low applicant quality** — lowest conversion rate from inquiry to approved Resident of any platform tested.
4. **Prospect drop-off** — many inquiries stopped after learning the property is professionally managed and requires a formal application process.

**Owner option:** Owners may post on Facebook Marketplace independently and forward inquiries to their Leasing Agent. Based on our experience, response rates after management contact are minimal.


:::info
**Response Template — Owner requests Facebook Marketplace listing**

Thanks for the suggestion! Sagareus tested Facebook Marketplace for over a year. We found it produced very low-quality leads and a high drop-off rate once prospects learned the property is professionally managed. Because Facebook Marketplace also requires posting from personal accounts, we no longer use it for listings — though you're welcome to post the property yourself and forward any inquiries if you'd like.

:::

## For Rent / For Lease Signs

Sagareus does not place "For Rent" or "For Lease" signs at properties. Testing confirmed they generate very few qualified leads while signaling the home is vacant — attracting break-in and squatter risk.

Sagareus relies on professional digital marketing and listing platforms, which consistently generate more qualified inquiries with less security risk.
