> **Situation:** Resident asks whether their neighbor is renewing their lease, moving out, or inquires about another Resident's lease status.


---

## Key Points

* **Privacy policy** — We do not disclose lease or renewal details of other Residents
* **Acknowledge concerns** — Take the Resident's feedback seriously and document internally
* **Offer alternatives** — If the Resident is considering not renewing because of a neighbor issue, offer to explore alternative unit options


---


:::warning
**We do not disclose lease or renewal details** of other Residents for privacy reasons. Residents may report concerns, and we will address those issues directly with the other party as appropriate.

:::

## Response

> **Subject:** Re: Neighbor Concern & Lease Renewal
>
> Hi \[Resident's First Name\],
>
> Thank you for sharing your concerns. I'm really sorry to hear the situation with your neighbor has affected your living experience; That's never what we want for our residents.
>
> We appreciate you taking the time to let us know, and I'll make sure your feedback is documented internally. While we're not able to share or confirm the lease status of other residents due to privacy policies, please know that we take Resident concerns seriously and will address any lease violations or disruptive behavior.
>
> We'd genuinely love for you to stay with us, and if you decide to renew, we're happy to explore alternative unit options as they become available. Just let us know if that's something you'd like us to keep an eye out for.
>
> Thank you again for your kind words about your experience overall — that means a lot to us.
>
> Best regards,

### Example Resident Questions

* "Is my neighbor moving out?"
* "Are they renewing their lease?"
* "I won't renew if that person is staying"
