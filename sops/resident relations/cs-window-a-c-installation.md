> **Situation:** Resident asks if they can install a window A/C unit.


---

## Key Points

* **Window A/C not allowed** — Can loosen, fall, and cause injury or property damage
* **Portable A/C recommended** — Effective, easy to move, simple to store, and can be resold at move-out
* **Guidelines** — Follow manufacturer venting and drip management; do not modify electrical, windows, or structure


---

## Response

> Hi \[Resident First Name\],
>
> Thanks for checking. I understand it's been warm. Window A/C units aren't allowed as they can loosen, fall, and cause injury or property damage.
>
> If you'd like extra cooling, the best option is a [**portable A/C unit**](https://www.amazon.com/BLACK-DECKER-BPACT12WT-Portable-Conditioner/dp/B01DLPUWGW/). They're effective, easy to move room-to-room, and simple to store in winter. At move-out, you can take it with you or resell it quickly on Facebook Marketplace.
>
> **Guidelines for Portable A/Cs**
> • Portable A/Cs are allowed; follow manufacturer venting and drip-management to prevent damage.
> • Do not modify electrical, windows, or the structure.
> • Filters and routine upkeep are the resident's responsibility.
>
> Best,
