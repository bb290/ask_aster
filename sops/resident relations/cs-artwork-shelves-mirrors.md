> **Situation:** Resident asks if they can hang artwork, shelves, or mirrors on the walls.


---

## Key Points

* **Lightweight decor allowed** — Small nails or tacks for framed art/photos are normal wear and tear; no charge at move-out
* **Shelves/mirrors allowed with warning** — Screws or anchors may result in drywall repair charges at move-out
* **Monkey/Gorilla Hooks recommended** — Least damage option for heavier items
* **No brick/tile/masonry** — Repair costs up to $5,000+; strongly discouraged


---

## Response

> Thank you for checking in. Below is Sagareus Wall Hanging & Installation Policy, pasted from our [Resident FAQ Page](https://www.sagareus.com/resident-faqs):
>
> **Wall Hanging & Installation Policy**
>
> **Allowed - Lightweight decor using small nails or tacks.** This is considered normal wear and tear and will not result in a charge at move-out. Examples include:
> • Small nail or tack holes for hanging framed art or photos
> • Adhesive picture hooks that can be cleanly removed
>
> **Allowed (with warning) – Shelves, mirrors, or heavy items that require screws or anchors.** These installations often result in larger holes or wall damage. You may be charged for drywall repairs at move-out. If you choose to install shelves or large wall-mounted mirrors:
> • Consider [Monkey or Gorilla Hooks](https://www.amazon.com/Monkey-Hook-Picture-Hanger-Office/dp/B001KVMNPI/) for least damage option
> • Understand that you'll be responsible for wall repair cost when you move out
>
> Avoid anchoring into tile, brick, or masonry. Anchor bolts drilled into brick, tile, or masonry surfaces can cause extensive damage, with repair costs ranging up to $5,000 or more. For this reason, mounting to brick or tile surfaces is **strongly discouraged**.
