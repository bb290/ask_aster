> **Situation:** Resident requests that a permanent A/C system be installed in the property (post move-in / during tenancy).


---

## Key Points

* **Owner decision** — At this time, the owner is not installing a permanent A/C system
* **Portable A/C recommended** — Same as window A/C scenario; recommend portable unit
* **Guidelines** — Follow manufacturer venting and drip management; do not modify electrical, windows, or structure


---

## Response

> Hi \[Resident First Name\],
>
> Thanks for reaching out. I understand it's been warm. At this time, the owner is **not** installing a permanent A/C system in the home.
>
> If you'd like additional cooling, your best option is to purchase a [**portable A/C unit**](https://www.amazon.com/BLACK-DECKER-BPACT12WT-Portable-Conditioner/dp/B01DLPUWGW/). They're effective, easy to move room-to-room, and simple to store in winter. At move-out, you can take it with you or resell it quickly on platforms like Facebook Marketplace.
>
> **Guidelines for Portable A/Cs**
> • Portable A/Cs are allowed; follow manufacturer venting and drip-management to prevent damage.
> • Do not modify electrical, windows, or the structure.
> • Filters and routine upkeep are the resident's responsibility.
>
> Best,
