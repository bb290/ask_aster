> **Situation:** Resident asks if they can mount a TV on the wall.


---

## Key Points

* **Professional installation required** — Must be installed by a licensed and insured handyman or contractor
* **Move-out charges** — Resident responsible for all wall repairs at move-out; typical drywall patch and paint costs $600–$1,000
* **No brick/tile/masonry** — Anchor bolts in these surfaces cause extensive damage; repair costs up to $5,000+


---

## Response

> Thank you for checking in. Below is Sagareus TV Wall Mount Installation Policy, pasted from our [Resident FAQ Page](https://www.sagareus.com/resident-faqs):
>
> **TV Wall Mount Installation Policy**
> Residents are permitted to install TV wall mounts **only under the following conditions** to ensure safety and avoid costly property damage:
>
> **Installation Requirements**
> TV mounts must be installed by a licensed and insured handyman or contractor. This requirement helps prevent:
> • Hitting electrical wiring or plumbing
> • Misalignment with wall studs
> • Improper anchoring that could cause the TV to fall, posing risk of injury and major property damage
>
> Proof of professional installation may be requested by management at any time.
>
> **Wall Damage & Move-Out Charges**
> Residents will be responsible for all wall repairs related to the removal of a TV mount at move-out. Typical drywall patch and paint repairs for wall-mounted TVs range between $600 – $1,000.
>
> Avoid anchoring into tile, brick, or masonry. Anchor bolts drilled into brick, tile, or masonry surfaces can cause extensive damage, with repair costs ranging up to $5,000 or more. For this reason, mounting to brick or tile surfaces is **strongly discouraged**.
