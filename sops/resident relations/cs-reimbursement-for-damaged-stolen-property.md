> **Situation:** Resident requests reimbursement for personal property that was damaged or stolen on the premises.


---

## Key Points

* **Management not responsible** — Per the Lease Agreement, management is not responsible for loss, theft, or damage of residents' personal property
* **Renter's insurance** — Required under the lease; Resident should file a claim with their insurance provider
* **Offer documentation support** — If Resident needs confirmation of the incident or property details for their claim, provide it promptly


---

## Response

> Hi \[Resident Name\],
>
> Thank you for letting us know about the issue with your personal belongings. We understand how frustrating this situation is and want to clarify how these matters are handled.
>
> Per your Lease Agreement, management is not responsible for the loss, theft, or damage of residents' personal property, whether inside the unit or in shared/common facilities such as laundry rooms, storage areas, hallways, or other common spaces. For this reason, reimbursement cannot be provided by management.
>
> As renter's insurance is required under your lease, this type of incident is typically covered by your policy. Please contact your insurance provider to file a claim for reimbursement. If you need documentation from us to support your claim (such as confirmation of the incident or property details), let us know and we'll provide it promptly.
>
> Best regards,

### Example Resident Questions

* "My belongings were damaged in the laundry room"
* "Someone stole my bike from the storage area"
* "Water damage ruined my furniture, will you pay for it?"
