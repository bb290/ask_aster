> **Situation:** Resident requests a waiver of a late fee or returned payment fee.


---

## Key Points

* **One-time courtesy** — First occurrence may be waived as a goodwill gesture to retain positive tenant relationships
* **Second+ occurrence** — Fee remains applied and must be paid in full with next rent payment
* **Applies to** — Late fees and returned payments (insufficient funds, invalid account, payment reversal)


---


:::tip
To promote goodwill and retain positive tenant relationships, we can extend a **one-time courtesy waiver** of late or returned payment fees. Repeated occurrences will not qualify for additional waivers.

:::

## Response

**First Occurrence (fee waived):**

> Hi \[Resident First Name\],
>
> Thanks for reaching out. As a one-time courtesy, we've waived the \[late/returned payment\] fee. Please ensure timely payment moving forward to avoid penalty fees.
>
> Thank you for your attention to this matter.
>
> Best,

**Second+ Occurrence (fee remains):**

> Hi \[Resident First Name\],
>
> Thanks for reaching out. We've reviewed your account, and since this is not the first occurrence, the \[late/returned payment\] fee will remain as outlined in your lease. Please ensure timely payment moving forward to avoid penalty fees.
>
> Thank you for your understanding.
>
> Best,

### Example Resident Questions

* "Can you waive my late fee?"
* "My payment bounced, can you remove the returned payment fee?"
* "This is the first time I've been late, please give me a break"
