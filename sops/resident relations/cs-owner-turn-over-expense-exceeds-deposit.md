> **Situation:** After the tenant moves out, the turn over estimate exceeds the available security deposit. The owner asks what happens next.


---

## Key Points

* **Deposit withholding is limited to damage exceeding normal wear and tear.** The determination of what qualifies is handled by the [Deposit Withholding procedure](../../docs/section-indexes/sops-2-process-deposit.md) — only damage beyond ordinary use is eligible.
* **Deposit is applied to outstanding balances first.** If the tenant has unpaid rent, unpaid utilities, or turn over repair expenses, we withhold from the deposit to cover those costs.
* **When expenses exceed the deposit**, the tenant still owes the owner for the remaining balance. If they don't pay within 60 days or establish a payment plan, the outstanding amount is forwarded to a 3rd party collection agency.
* **Legal deadline** — The deposit statement must be sent to the resident within 30 days of move-out.
* **Collection agency recovery rates** — Once submitted to a collection agency, the chance of recovery is very low. Recoveries rarely exceed 30% of the total amount owed.

## Owner Inquiry

Reply to the owner's email thread.

> **Subject: RE: \[Original Subject\]**
>
> Hi \[Owner Name\],
>
> The resident's security deposit is applied toward eligible charges — specifically damage that goes beyond normal wear and tear, along with any unpaid rent or utilities. We document everything with move-in/move-out inspections, photos, and vendor invoices to support each charge.
>
> When the total exceeds the deposit, the remaining balance is billed to the former resident. If it goes unpaid, we escalate to a third-party collection agency on your behalf.
>
> Let me know if you have any other questions.
