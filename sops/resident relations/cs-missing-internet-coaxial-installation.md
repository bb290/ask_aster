> **Situation:** Resident reports there are no existing or dysfunctional internet connection options (missing coaxial outlets or wiring) in their unit.


---

## Key Points

* **Resident can schedule installation** — Authorized to use preferred ISP (commonly Xfinity, Quantum Fiber, or CenturyLink)
* **Reimbursement up to $250** — If there's a one-time fee to bring the physical line to the unit, Sagareus will credit up to $250 upon receipt of the bill
* **No structural modifications** — Technicians may install hardware/wiring but Resident must not modify electrical, windows, or structure


---

## Response

> **Subject:** Internet Installation Authorization // \[Property Address\]
>
> Hi \[Resident Name\],
>
> Thank you for letting us know about the lack of internet connection options in your unit. It's not uncommon for some buildings to be missing coaxial outlets or wiring if service was never established by a previous occupant.
>
> You are welcome to schedule installation with your preferred internet service provider — most commonly Xfinity, Quantum Fiber, or CenturyLink. Please provide them with the following authorization for any hardware, wiring, or equipment installation. Depending on the provider you choose, there may be a one time fee to bring the physical line to your unit. If this occurs, please send a copy of your bill, and we will issue a full credit for the cost up to $250.
>
> **To Whom It May Concern**
> As Property Manager, I authorize technicians to install, upgrade, or otherwise make available any necessary hardware, wiring, and/or equipment to provide the desired internet service chosen by **\[Resident Name(s)\]** at **\[Property Address\]**.
>
> Best regards,
