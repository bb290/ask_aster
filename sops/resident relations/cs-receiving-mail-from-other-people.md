> **Situation:** New Resident reports receiving mail addressed to previous Residents or the property owner.


---

## Key Points

* **Identify the addressee** — Ask Resident to check who the mail is addressed to, or send a photo of the envelope
* **If addressed to Owner** — Contact owner to ask if they want to retrieve it or have Resident dispose; remind about mail forwarding
* **If addressed to previous Resident** — Contact previous Resident with same options; remind about mail forwarding
* **If no contact info for previous Resident** — Ask current Resident to write "Return to sender — no longer live here" on the envelope and place back in mailbox


---


:::info
This usually happens when a new Resident moves in. Use the response template below and find out if the mails are from the owners or previous Residents.

:::

## Response

> Thanks for reaching out. Could you please check who the mail is addressed to, or if easier, send us a photo of the envelope?
>
> It is not uncommon for leftover mail to arrive shortly after move-in. Once we know who the mail belongs to, we'll either contact the owner or ask the previous Resident to set up forwarding. Feel free to discard any unsolicited ads or spam.

## Email Template — Notify Owner

> The Resident at \[Property Address\] reached out after receiving mail with your name on it. Please let us know if you would like us to arrange for you to retrieve it, or if you are comfortable having the Resident dispose of it. Reminder to set up mail forwarding with USPS if you have not already.

## Email Template — Notify Previous Resident

> The new Residents at \[Property Address\] reached out after receiving mail with your name on it. Please let us know if you would like us to arrange for you to retrieve it, or if you are comfortable having the Resident dispose of it. Reminder to set up mail forwarding with USPS if you have not already.

## Email Template — No Previous Resident Contact Info

> Thank you for reaching out. For mails received addressed to the previous Residents, please just place it back in the mailbox and write on the envelope: ***"Return to sender – no longer live here."*** This helps the postal service redirect the mail appropriately and should reduce future deliveries for former residents.

### Example Resident Questions

* "I'm getting mail for someone who doesn't live here"
* "The previous tenant's mail keeps coming"
* "What do I do with the owner's mail?"
