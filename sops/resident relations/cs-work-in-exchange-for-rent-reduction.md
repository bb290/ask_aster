> **Situation:** Resident offers to perform maintenance or improvement work on the property in exchange for a rent reduction or compensation.


---

## Key Points

* **Not allowed** — Sagareus does not allow Residents to perform work in exchange for reduced rent or any form of compensation
* **Liability risk** — Residents are often not licensed or insured, creating landlord liability
* **Quality concerns** — Work may not meet professional standards
* **Exception — licensed professional** — Sagareus may subcontract them as a vendor only if they provide W9 and insurance, and invoice Sagareus directly (never as rent credit)
* **Exception — small basic tasks** — Sagareus may reimburse materials only for replacing bulbs, furnace filters, weed spray, etc. with receipt and prior approval


---


:::warning
Sagareus does **not** allow Residents to perform work in exchange for reduced rent or any form of compensation.

* Residents are often not licensed or insured—creates landlord liability.
* Work quality may not meet professional standards.
* Allowing this shifts the landlord/Resident relationship into a problematic "favor" dynamic.

:::

## Response

> Thank you for your offer to help. While we appreciate your willingness to contribute, our policy does not allow us to offer rent discounts in exchange for Resident work. This is to ensure all maintenance is performed to professional and insurance standards.
>
> If you are interested in helping with small tasks like replacing light bulbs or furnace filters, let us know and we can discuss reimbursing material costs with a receipt.
>
> Thanks again for reaching out and for being proactive.

## Action


1. Document communication
2. Task complete
