> **Situation:** Resident asks about modifying the landscaping, patio, or vegetation on the property.


---

## Key Points

* **Owner approval required** — Request must be forwarded to the property owner
* **Details needed** — Scope of work, photos/sketches, whether a professional landscaper will be hired


---

## Resident Inquiry

> Thanks for reaching out! We will forward your landscaping request to the property owner for review. Please include:
> • The scope of work or changes you'd like to make
> • Any photos or sketches
> • Whether you'll be hiring a professional landscaper
>
> We will confirm once the owner provides direction.


---

## Owner Inquiry

> **Subject: Landscaping Request // \[Property Address\]**
> **To:** \[Owner Name/Email\]
>
> Hi \[Owner Name\],
>
> We received a request from the resident at \[Property Address\] to modify the landscaping. Details below:
>
> • Scope of work: \[Description\]
> • Photos/sketches: \[Attached/None Provided\]
> • Professional landscaper: \[Yes/No\]
>
> Please let us know if you'd like to approve or deny this request.
>
> Thank you,


---

## Email Template — Approved

> **Subject: Landscaping Request Approved // \[Property Address\]**
>
> Hi \[Resident Name\],
>
> Great news — the property owner has approved your landscaping request. Please let us know if you have any questions.
>
> Thank you,

## Email Template — Rejected

> **Subject: Landscaping Request Update // \[Property Address\]**
>
> Hi \[Resident Name\],
>
> Thank you for your landscaping request at \[Property Address\]. Unfortunately, the property owner has decided not to approve the request at this time.
>
> If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to reach out.
>
> Regards,

### Example Resident Questions

* "Can I plant a garden in the backyard?"
* "I'd like to add some plants to the patio"
* "Can I remove the dead bush in the front yard?"
