## Owner Deficit Policy + Procedures


:::warning
When an owner's account deficit balance exceeds **$2,000**, Sagareus' accounting and management teams take immediate action to protect the financial stability of the property and the company.

:::

### Notification & Communication

Owners are notified in writing (email) once the deficit crosses the $2,000 threshold.

### Required Action

Owners must bring the account back to a positive balance within **10 business days** of notice.

### Escalation & Restrictions


:::warning
Failure to transfer funds within **10 business days** will result in the **suspension of all services** until the account is brought back into compliance.

Failure to transfer funds within 20 business days will result in the **termination of the management agreement**.

:::


---

### Email Templates: Initial 10-Day Warning, 5-Day Warning, 2-Day Warning

(Three escalating warning templates for owners with account deficits exceeding $2,000.)

## Owner Contributions

### Recording Owner Paid Bills

Create bill (memo prefix "Owner paid"), mark as paid, record Owner Contribution.

### Requesting an Owner Contribution for Large Vendor Invoice

Create Owner Contribution task in Asana, request via email, confirm funds, make vendor payment.

## Temp Loans

### Requesting an Owner Contribution for Temp Loan

Temp loans from Sagareus are sometimes issued to owner's accounts to fund utility and vendor payments on time.

### Temporarily Loaning Owner Operating Account

Accounting > Banking > Record Other Transaction. Memo: "Temp Loan".

### Returning Loan From Owner Operating Account

Verify temp loan amount, review owner's operating account, transfer back to Sagareus account. Memo: "Return Temp Loan".


:::tip
* Owner Operating Account should only be loaned up to $1000 at a time, unless authorized
* Utilities have priority over vendor bills
* Reference Sagareus Balance Sheet before disbursement to verify if funds need to be returned

:::


---

## Correcting Mistransferred Funds

Identify accounts involved, identify bill involved, separate accounts with funding issues, record the correction.
