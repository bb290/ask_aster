**Scenario:** An owner asks about a $75 "Notice servicing fee" charge on their statement, or asks why they're being charged for notice delivery.

## Background

Washington state law ([RCW 59.12.040](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.12.040)) requires that landlord notices be served either in person or by certified mail posted from within Washington state.

## Response

*Hi [Owner Name],*

*Thank you for reaching out. The notice servicing fee of $75 covers the cost of properly serving legally required notices to your tenant(s) in compliance with Washington state law. This includes:*

* *Printing the notice document*
* *Serving the notice in person at the property*
* *Documenting a certificate of service*
* *Mailing the notice to the tenant via USPS Certified Mail*

*Under Washington state law (*[*RCW 59.12.040*](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.12.040)*), landlord notices must be served either personally or by certified mail posted from within Washington. This process requires a field visit to the property and certified mailing for each notice served.*

*Effective April 1, 2026, this fee applies to all properties to cover the complexity of Washington's notice servicing requirements. For properties that have a renewal fee, the notice servicing cost is already included — there is no additional charge.*

*Please let us know if you have any further questions.*
