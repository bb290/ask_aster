> **Situation:** A resident contacts Sagareus asking why they received multiple notices — in-person visits, certified mail, and/or email — about their renewal or rent increase.


---

## Key Points

* **Washington State and local laws require multiple forms of service.** Rent increase and renewal notices must be delivered via both certified mail **and** in-person service to comply with updated regulations.
* **This is legally required, not optional.** Sagareus cannot reduce the number of notices without violating compliance requirements.
* **Residents do not need to respond to rent increase notices.** The increase takes effect automatically. Renewal offers do require a signed response.
* **Expect a similar process next year.** The notice requirements are ongoing legal obligations.


---

## Resident Inquiry

Reply to the original email thread.

> Thank you for reaching out. The letter you received includes a copy of the upcoming rent renewal and was delivered as part of our efforts to comply with updated Washington State and local notice requirements. These changes now mandate stricter procedures for serving lease renewals, rent increases, and related notices, including certified mail **and** in-person service. We understand that receiving certified letters or in-person visits may feel excessive, and we sincerely apologize for any disruption or confusion.
