> **Situation:** An owner asks if their month-to-month tenant can be moved back onto a fixed-term lease.


---

## Key Points

* **Once a lease converts to MTM, it cannot be converted back to a termed lease.** Washington law prohibits landlords from terminating MTM leases except for specific legal reasons — this means there is no mechanism to force a new fixed term.
* **Only the Resident can decide when to end an MTM tenancy.** The landlord cannot compel the tenant to sign a new fixed-term lease.
* **Rent increases still apply.** MTM tenants receive annual rent increases on the same schedule as termed leases, so the owner does not lose income growth.
* **Prevention is key.** Always renew or terminate a lease before it converts to MTM to maintain owner flexibility.
