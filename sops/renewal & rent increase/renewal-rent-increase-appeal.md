> **Situation:** A resident contacts Sagareus to dispute or appeal the rent increase amount, often citing financial hardship, maintenance issues, or market comparisons. The appeal is evaluated using market data only — not resident complaints.


---

## Key Points

* **Appeals are approved only with documented market data.** Sagareus does not reduce rent increases based on resident complaints alone.
* **If approved, the maximum reduction is 50%** of the original increase.
* **Maintenance issues are handled separately.** If the resident raises maintenance concerns during the appeal, route those to the maintenance team. Maintenance does not affect the rent increase procedure.
* **The rent increase notice remains valid** regardless of the appeal outcome. The increase takes effect on the specified date unless a revised notice is issued.


---

## Resident Inquiry

When a resident reaches out to appeal, direct them to submit their appeal in writing via email. Then follow the procedure below to evaluate.


---

## Procedure

**IF rent increase was originally sent to the owner for approval:**


1. Pull comparable
   * **IF Multifamily** — Pull latest rent roll sorted by Rent, attach picture to the Appeal Task
2. Approval decisions are based on market data exclusively:
   * **IF** rent is below average rent from the comparable: **NOT approved**
   * **IF** rent is NOT the most expensive unit in the building (for the room count): **NOT approved**
3. **IF approved**, we can offer reduced rent increase by **50%**
