## This Report Is Public-Facing


:::warning
**The move-out inspection report is a public-facing document.** It is shared with property owners, outgoing tenants, vendors, and in some cases is presented as evidence in court during deposit disputes.

All written comments must be **factual, professional, and neutral in tone.** Describe what you observe — not what you assume happened. Avoid subjective language, blame, or emotional phrasing.

**Do:** *"Carpet has visible staining in the living room near the entry. See photo."* **Don't:** *"Tenant trashed the carpet."*

:::

## Completing the Inspection

**Every question in the Inspector app must be answered — no exceptions.** Do not skip fields or leave items blank, even if the area appears to be in good condition. Unanswered questions create gaps that weaken our documentation during deposit disputes and make it harder for vendors to scope turnover work.

If an item is in acceptable condition, mark it accordingly and move on. If it needs attention, mark it and add detail (see [Marking "Needs Attention"](#marking-needs-attention) below).

### Functional Testing

In addition to documenting visual condition, you must **test the following during the inspection**:

* **Plumbing** — Run every faucet (hot and cold), flush every toilet, check under sinks for leaks
* **Lights & Electrical** — Test all light switches, ceiling fans, and accessible outlets
* **Doors & Locks** — Open and close all interior and exterior doors; test all locks and deadbolts
* **Windows** — Confirm each window opens, closes, and locks (captured as part of the [3-photo window requirement](#window-documentation-3-required-photos))
* **Garbage disposal** — Run it briefly to confirm function

If anything does not work, mark it "Needs Attention" and note the issue. Do not assume it will be caught later.

## Best Practices

### Photos

Good photos are the backbone of a defensible inspection. Every photo should tell a clear story about *what* you're documenting and *where* it is in the unit.

**Wide Angle First, Then Close-Up**

For every area or item you photograph:


1. **Wide-angle context shot** — Step back and capture the full wall, corner, or room so the viewer knows exactly where the item is located in the unit.
2. **Close-up detail shot** — Move in close to capture the specific condition, damage, or item of concern.

This pairing ensures that anyone reviewing the report — owner, vendor, or judge — can identify the exact location without guessing.

### Window Documentation (3 Required Photos)

Every window must be documented with **three separate photos**:

| Photo | What It Captures |
|-------|------------------|
| **Blind down** | Condition of the window covering (blinds, shades) in the closed position |
| **Blind up** | Condition of the window glass, frame, sill, and locks with the covering raised |
| **Window open** | Functionality of the window — confirms it opens, closes, and locks properly |

These three shots are forcing functions: they ensure we verify the condition of both the window coverings *and* the window itself. Skipping any of the three leaves a gap in documentation.

### Keys, Remotes & Access Devices

During the inspection, verify and document the return of all access devices issued at move-in:

* **Keys** — unit keys, mailbox keys, common area keys
* **Garage door remotes** — test each to confirm they work
* **Fobs / gate cards** — if applicable

If any items are missing, note it in the inspection report and flag it in your move-out task. Missing items may result in replacement charges to the outgoing tenant.

### Exterior, Balcony, Patio & Storage

If the unit includes any of the following, they are part of the inspection — do not skip them:

* **Balcony / patio** — surface condition, railing, debris, personal items left behind
* **Yard** — landscaping condition, trash, damage to fencing or gates
* **Garage / carport** — floor stains, damage, items left behind
* **Storage unit / closet** — emptied and clean

## Marking "Needs Attention"

When an item is marked **"Needs Attention,"** you must provide enough detail for a vendor to act on it without contacting you for clarification.


:::info
Vendors receive the move-out report and are instructed to *"address items marked Needs Attention."* To be successful, they need specifics — not just a photo.

* **Be specific about location:** Which windowsill? In which room? Left side or right side?
* **Pair with photos:** Include a wide-angle shot for context and a close-up of the issue.

:::


:::tip
**Example:** Instead of *"windowsill damage"* → write *"Windowsill in primary bedroom, left window — paint chipping and water stain visible. See photos."*

:::

## Cleanliness Grading Scale

Assign a cleanliness grade during the inspection to standardize how we assess and report unit condition. This ensures consistent communication between Leasing, Operations, Vendors, Residents, and Owners.

### A+ — Immaculate / Move-In Ready


:::tip
*"The unit is fully ready for move-in. No further cleaning needed."*

:::

* Entire unit is spotless and odor-free
* All appliances clean inside and out, including microwave, oven, fridge, and dishwasher
* All kitchen and bathroom cabinets/drawers fully cleaned inside and out
* Surfaces, floors, and fixtures are visually flawless and clean to the touch

### B+ — Clean / Light Touch-Up Needed


:::tip
*"A quick clean will bring this to full move-in condition."*

:::

* Unit is mostly clean with minor dust or smudges
* Appliances deep cleaned
* Cabinets/drawers appear clean but may benefit from a quick check and wipe-down

### C+ — Fair / Needs Standard Cleaning


:::tip
*"No major mess, but a full move-out clean is needed to meet move-in standards."*

:::

* Mostly cleared out, but with surface dust, smudges, or pet hair
* Appliances appear used / not clean
* Cabinets and drawers empty but may have visible crumbs, spills, residue, or debris

### D+ — Poor / Needs Deep Cleaning


:::tip
*"The unit is not ready for any turnover work. Deep clean needed before repairs or photos."*

:::

* Heavy dirt, stains, and residue on floors, counters, and major surfaces
* Trash, pet hair, or left-behind items present
* Appliances greasy, stained, or with food debris inside
* Cabinets and drawers contain crumbs, spills, or personal items

### F+ — Unacceptable / Hazardous


:::warning
*"The unit is unsafe or unsanitary — protective gear may be needed to enter."*

:::

* Major health hazards present (e.g., pest infestation, mold, spoiled food, biohazards)
* Severe filth, trash, or odors throughout the unit
* Appliances caked with grime or rotting food, posing health concerns
* Kitchen/bath cabinets and drawers heavily soiled, sticky, or containing trash/personal items
* Immediate deep cleaning and possibly specialty remediation required
