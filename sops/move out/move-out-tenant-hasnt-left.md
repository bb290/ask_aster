**When to Use This Page**

> *Use this page if:*
>
> * *you showed up and the tenant is still there*
> * *keys aren't in the lockbox*
> * *you can see belongings still in the unit*
> * *you're not sure if the property is vacant and safe to enter*

**Situation Summary** 

Sometimes you arrive for a scheduled move-out inspection and the resident hasn't fully vacated — or it's unclear whether they have. This page covers what to do to protect yourself legally, document the situation, and get things back on track without entering an occupied unit.


---

## What to Do When the Unit Appears Occupied


1. Before entering, assess the situation: are keys missing from the lockbox? Can you see belongings through the window? Is anyone present?
2. **Do not enter.** Even if the resident was supposed to be out, you cannot enter an occupied home without 24-hour written notice. If you're not sure whether it's occupied — don't enter.
3. Document everything in Asana immediately.
   * Example: *"Keys not in box, able to see garbage bags and a few boxes from the window — property looks occupied. Called and texted resident for update. Attempting to reschedule move-out for tomorrow."*
4. Call and text the resident to find out:
   * Do they need more time?
   * Do they still intend to move out?
5. Update the Move-Out task due date to the following day.

> ⚠️ If the resident says they are **no longer moving out** → document in Asana and notify your Team Leader. 
>
> \
> ⚠️ If the resident says they need **more time or a new move-out date** → follow the \[Modified Move-Out Date SOP\].
