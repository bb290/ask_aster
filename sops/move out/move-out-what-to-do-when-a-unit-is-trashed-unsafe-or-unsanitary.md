**When to Use This Page**

> *Use this page if:*
>
> * *you arrived for a move-out and the unit is full of trash*
> * *you can't access walls or floors due to clutter*
> * *there are signs of bugs, drug use, biohazards, or other safety concerns*
> * *you're not sure if it's safe to conduct the inspection*

**Situation Summary** 

If you arrive for a move-out inspection and the unit isn't safe or accessible for a formal inspection, do not proceed. Personal safety comes first. This page covers how to document the situation, get a trash-out vendor dispatched, and reschedule the formal inspection.


---

## What to Do When the Unit Is Unsafe or Unsanitary


1. **Do not proceed with the full inspection.** If the unit appears unsafe or unsanitary, stop at the entrance.
2. Do not touch or move any items.
3. From the entrance or a safe vantage point, take wide-angle photos documenting:
   * Excessive trash or clutter
   * Areas where walls or floors are inaccessible
   * Any visible safety concerns (pests, needles, suspicious substances, biohazards)
4. Create a **"Unit Trash Out Needed"** task in Asana:
   * Attach all documentation photos
   * Add brief notes describing what you observed
   * Assign to the Maintenance Coordinator
   * Set due date to today
   * Tag both the Maintenance Coordinator and Operations Manager
5. Update the Move-Out Inspection task due date to **3 days out** to allow time for the trash-out to be completed.
6. Monitor the trash-out task. Once the Maintenance Coordinator confirms it's done (they'll tag you), reassign the Move-Out Inspection to yourself and complete it.


---

## Escalation

Tag the Maintenance Coordinator and Operations Manager on the Trash Out task. This triggers priority scheduling of a third-party trash-out vendor.
