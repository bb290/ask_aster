> *Use this page if:*
>
> * *a tenant refuses to leave the key in the lockbox*
> * *a tenant is demanding to be present for the move-out inspection*
> * *you need to manage a tenant walkthrough before doing the real inspection*

**Situation Summary** 

Occasionally a resident insists on being present for their move-out inspection or won't leave a key in the lockbox. The goal is to satisfy their request, get them off-site, and then complete the real inspection independently — which is required for it to be thorough and accurate.


---

## How to Handle a Tenant Who Wants to Be Present


1. Agree to meet the resident on-site to collect the keys.
2. Walk through the unit with them, going through the motions of a general walkthrough.
3. Take note of anything they specifically want documented.
4. Reassure them: your job today is only to document the property's current condition. Nothing from this walkthrough directly determines their charges. The Turn Over Coordinator compares the Move-In and Move-Out Condition Reports, accounts for normal wear and tear, and makes reasonable deductions. Let them know they can find dispute instructions on the Move-Out Procedures page.
5. Once they're satisfied, lock up normally — make it clear to the resident that the move-out is complete.
6. Walk to your car and wait approximately 5 minutes.
7. Once the resident has left, re-enter the property and complete the full move-out inspection unattended.
