## Requirement for deposit withholding


:::info
An expense line item is eligible for deposit withholding if the following requirements are met:

* **Move-in inspection report** is available and signed by the Resident
* **Prior condition** supported by documentation - picture or text
* **After condition** supported by documentation - picture or text
* **Itemized invoice** from vendor confirming the work is done

:::

### DO NOT CHARGE:


:::warning
* Wear resulting from ordinary use. This is defined by [RCW 59.18.030(39)](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.18.030):
  * "Wear resulting from ordinary use of the premises" means deterioration that results from the intended use of a dwelling unit, including breakage or malfunction due to age or deteriorated condition. Such wear does not include deterioration that results from negligence, carelessness, accident, or abuse of the premises, fixtures, equipment, appliances, or furnishings by the Resident, immediate family member, occupant, or guest.
* Carpet Cleaning or replacement
  * Per [RCW 59.18.595](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=59.18.595) (effective 7/23/2023), landlords cannot charge for professional carpet cleaning at move-out unless the carpet is damaged beyond ordinary use.
  * Keep in mind that routine cleaning is part of "ordinary use." If it looks like the Resident has never vacuumed, you can certainly charge for cleaning.

:::
