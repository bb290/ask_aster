> **Situation:** A resident moved in or out mid-billing cycle, and the utility charge must be prorated so they are only billed for the days their lease was active during the service period.


---

## Key Points

* **Use the Utility Calculator** — all proration math runs through the [Utility Calculator spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1rWLTFUl8htA2_pxsXOZ-X0WOELmX7-Va_Eze5WnX0vU/edit?gid=0#gid=0).
* **Exclude late fees** — only the service total (minus late fees) is used in the calculation.
* **Move-in memo highlights lease start to service end** — the memo should note the beginning of the lease through the end of the service period on the bill.
* **Move-out uses the last paid bill as an estimate** — since the final bill has not yet arrived, prorate using the most recent paid bill and label the charge as estimated.


---

## Procedure

### Move In


1. Open the [Utility Calculator](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1rWLTFUl8htA2_pxsXOZ-X0WOELmX7-Va_Eze5WnX0vU/edit?gid=0#gid=0).
2. After receiving the bill for pass through:
   * Enter the service dates from the bill into the calculator.
   * Enter the service total (exclude any late fees).
   * The calculator will return the per-day amount.
   * Count the number of days the lease was active during the service period.
   * Multiply the per-day amount by the number of active lease days.
   * Add the resulting charge to the tenant's ledger and note the service period in the memo.
     * The memo should highlight the beginning of the lease and the end of the service period noted on the bill used in the calculation.

### Move Out


1. Open the [Utility Calculator](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1rWLTFUl8htA2_pxsXOZ-X0WOELmX7-Va_Eze5WnX0vU/edit?gid=0#gid=0).
2. Locate the last paid bill for each utility subject to pass through.
3. For each utility, calculate the prorated charge. The last paid bill is used to estimate the next charge and the amount the tenant owes.
   * Enter the service dates from the bill into the calculator.
   * Enter the service total (exclude any late fees).
   * The calculator will return the per-day amount.
   * Count the number of days the lease was active during the service period.
   * Multiply the per-day amount by the number of active lease days.
   * Add the resulting charge to the tenant's ledger and note the service period in the memo.
     * The memo should highlight the beginning of the service period and the end of the lease. Include "Estimated *Utility* | *dates*."
