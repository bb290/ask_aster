> **Situation:** A tenant is moving in and gas and/or electricity needs to be set up in their name. This applies when the utility company allows individual tenant accounts at the property.


---

## Key Points

* **Gas and electric only** — These can typically be placed directly in the tenant's name. Water accounts generally cannot be transferred to tenants.
* **Owner must agree** — Confirm the owner approves tenant-direct billing before proceeding
* **7-day window** — Tenants are expected to set up their own accounts within 7 days of lease start. Sagareus assists when needed.
* **Tenant info required** — Full name, phone number, driver's license (with state), date of birth, and email address


---

## Procedure

### 1. Confirm Tenant Details

Collect from the tenant:

* Full legal name
* Phone number
* Driver's license number and issuing state
* Date of birth
* Email address

### 2. Call the Utility Company

> **You:** Hi, I'm calling to set up a new account for a tenant at [Property Address]. The tenant's name is [Tenant Name]. What information do you need from me to proceed?

**Utility:** They will ask for tenant information.

> **You:** The tenant's information is: [Full Name], [Phone Number], [Driver's License Number — State], Date of Birth [DOB].

**Utility:** They will provide details on how to complete setup — could be:

* Completing setup over the phone
* Online portal link
* Sending in an application

### 3. Follow Their Process

Complete whatever steps the utility company requires. Document each step in Asana.

### 4. Confirm Activation

Call back to confirm the account is active and ask for:

* Service start date
* Deposit amount (if any)
* First bill due date
* Any notes to share with the tenant

### 5. Email the Tenant

Use the template below to confirm setup.

### 6. Document in Asana

Create a subtask with:

* Tenant name
* Account number
* Service type (electric, gas)
* Service start date
* Deposit amount (if any)
* First bill due date
* Email sent to tenant: Yes / No
* Date completed
