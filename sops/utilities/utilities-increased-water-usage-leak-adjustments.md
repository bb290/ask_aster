> **Situation:** A property shows a significant spike in water usage — detected during bill input (current bill exceeds the previous by 50%+), via a usage chart anomaly, a utility company notice, or an owner complaint about high consumption.


---

## Key Points

* **Trigger threshold** — Current water bill exceeds the previous bill by more than 50%, or another indicator flags abnormal usage
* **Task creation required** — Open an "Increase in Water Usage // [Property Address]" templated task in the Finance & Office project and attach the triggering evidence
* **Per-day usage data drives diagnosis** — Request daily consumption records from the utility company to distinguish a one-time spike from a persistent leak
* **Leak credit window** — After repairs are completed, request a leak adjustment promptly; timelines vary by provider (Tacoma allows one adjustment every 18 months)


---

## Procedure


1. Identify the trigger (bill spike, usage chart, utility notice, or owner complaint)
2. Create an "Increase in Water Usage // [Property Address]" task from the template in the Finance & Office project; attach the trigger evidence (bill, notice, or screenshot)
3. **Get Per-Day Usage Data from Utility Company** (assigned to Utility Coordinator)
   * Check the utility account online or email the utility company using the template below
4. Once daily usage data is received, review for signs of a persistent leak (e.g., meter never reaches zero overnight)
5. If a leak is confirmed, coordinate repairs through Operations
6. After repairs are completed and invoiced, request a leak credit from the utility company using the second email template below

## Email Template — Request Per-Day Usage Data

> **To:** [Utility Company]
>
> **Attach:** PMA agreement, original notice or bill showing the usage increase
>
> [Utility Company],
>
> We are responding to a significant increase in water usage at [Property Address]. Could you help us verify whether this indicates a persistent leak or a one-time spike?
>
> Do you have per-day usage data available for this property? Do you see any signs of a persistent leak?
>
> Sagareus is the property manager for this property (PMA attached) and the account holder.
>
> Thank you.

## Email Template — Request Leak Credit After Repairs

> **To:** [Utility Company]
>
> **Attach:** PMA agreement, original notice or bill, repair invoice
>
> [Utility Company],
>
> We responded to a significant increase in water usage at [Property Address], and a leak was detected. Attached are the repair invoice and the bill showing the initial usage spike.
>
> We are requesting a leak credit for this account. Could you provide per-day usage data for our records? What is the standard timeline for reviewing and approving a leak credit?
>
> Sagareus is the property manager for this property (PMA attached) and the account holder. Please let us know if anything else is needed to process this request.
>
> Thank you.

## Tacoma Public Utilities — Leak Adjustment Process

**Contact:** CService@CityofTacoma.org | 253-502-8600


1. Receive the most recent usage report to confirm a possible leak (if the water meter does not reach zero daily, an active leak is likely)
   * Access the account online at [mytpu.org](https://mytpu.org)
   * If online access is unavailable, contact TPU directly and request usage report screenshots (daily breakdown for the past week)
2. Once reports are received, assign the task to the Operations Manager to review and determine whether repairs are needed
3. After confirming repairs are complete and invoices are **paid**, email TPU copies of the invoices along with the leak detection dates and repair completion dates
4. Expect the adjustment to be applied to the next 1–2 billing cycles

> **Note:** Each account is eligible for one leak adjustment every 18 months.
