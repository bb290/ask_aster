## What Is the Sewer Capacity Charge?

A Sewer Capacity Charge (SCC) is a one-time infrastructure fee assessed when a property first connects to the regional sewer system. It funds system expansions — treatment plants, interceptors, and related upgrades.

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Billed by** | King County (or regional wastewater agency) |
| **Cadence** | Typically quarterly |
| **Duration** | Up to ~15 years after initial connection |
| **Transfers with property** | Yes — obligation remains until paid in full |
| **Separate from monthly sewer bills** | Yes   |


---

## Who Pays?

SCC is a capital infrastructure charge, not a metered utility. It is always the **owner's responsibility** and must not be billed to tenants.


---

## Can Sagareus Manage the SCC Account?

No. Agencies like King County do not permit third-party account management. The account must remain in the owner's name. However, Sagareus can still process payment on the owner's behalf.


---

## How to Have Sagareus Pay


1. Forward the bill (PDF or photo) to **accounting@sagareus.com**
2. Sagareus codes and pays the charge from property operating funds
3. The charge appears on the owner's statement

> **Tip:** Consider opting into e-billing and setting up auto-forwarding to accounting@sagareus.com so bills are never missed.
