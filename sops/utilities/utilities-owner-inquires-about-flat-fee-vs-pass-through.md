> **Situation:** An owner questions why Sagareus uses flat-fee utility billing instead of percentage pass-through, expressing concern that they won't recover the full utility cost.


---

## Key Points

* **Flat fee is Sagareus policy** for all properties with shared/master meters. This is not optional per property.
* **Simplifies accounting.** A fixed charge eliminates manual bill splitting, reduces errors, and prevents missed pass-throughs.
* **Avoids Seattle RUB compliance risk.** Variable pass-through in Seattle (3+ units) triggers SMC 7.25, increasing liability and giving tenants additional dispute avenues.
* **Covers excessive usage.** The lease addendum includes EV charging surcharges and a clause for usage above normal residential consumption.
* **Fee is data-driven.** Calculated from the prior 12 months of actual utility costs.

## Owner Inquiry

Reply to the owner's email thread.

#### Email Template -- Flat Fee Policy Explanation

> **Subject:** RE: [Original Subject]
>
> Sagareus uses a fixed monthly utility fee for all properties with shared or master-metered utilities. The fee is calculated from the prior 12 months of actual utility costs, so it closely reflects what Residents have been paying.
>
> We moved to this approach for a few reasons. A flat fee eliminates manual bill splitting each month, which is the most common source of missed charges and accounting mistakes on both ends. It also avoids triggering Seattle's Third Party Billing Regulation (SMC 7.25), which applies to variable pass-through billing in multifamily buildings and adds disclosure requirements that give Residents a formal avenue to dispute charges.
>
> Our lease also includes protections for unusual situations. There's a surcharge for EV charging and a clause that allows us to pass through excess costs if a Resident's usage substantially exceeds normal residential consumption.
>
> This is our standard policy across all managed properties. We're happy to share the utility audit data for your property if you'd like to review the numbers.
>
> Thank you,
