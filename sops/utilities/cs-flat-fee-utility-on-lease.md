> **Situation:** Owner or Resident asks about the flat fee utility charge listed on their lease.


---

## Key Points

* **Policy explanation** — Sagareus requires a fixed monthly utility fee in properties with shared or master meters where submetering is not feasible
* **Benefits** — Predictable billing, fewer disputes, simple and clear for Residents
* **Coverage** — Typically covers water, sewer, garbage (and master-metered gas/electric if applicable); separately metered utilities remain Resident's responsibility
* **Proration** — Fees are prorated at move-in/out; adjustments made prospectively with written notice; no monthly "true-up" based on usage
* **Escalation** — If owner or Resident pushes back, loop in Accounting Manager (Cooper)


---

## Response

> **Subject:** Lease Utility Billing Clarification
>
> Hi \[Name\],
>
> Thank you for your message and for reviewing the lease closely.
>
> Sagareus requires a fixed monthly utility fee in properties with shared or master meters where submetering is not feasible or cost-effective. The utility fee listed in your lease reflects this policy. This approach provides predictable billing, minimizes disputes, and streamlines collections for a better resident experience. The flat fee is always disclosed in both the listing and the lease so residents can plan with full transparency.
>
> **Flat-Fee Utility Billing**
> • **Predictable & Autopay-Friendly:** The same amount each month, reducing missed or incorrect payments.
> • **Fewer Disputes:** No splitting bills between neighbors or disputes over usage.
> • **Simple & Clear:** Typically covers water, sewer, garbage (and master-metered gas/electric, if applicable). Separately metered utilities remain the resident's responsibility.
> • **Proration & Notice:** Fees are prorated at move-in/out, and any adjustments are made prospectively with written notice. We do not "true-up" monthly based on usage.
> • **Periodic Review:** Fees are reviewed at turnover and lease renewal.
>
> Best regards,
