> **Situation:** A utility bill has been received and entered in Buildium. The bill needs to be paid, the payment recorded, and the credit card reimbursed from the property's bank account.


---

## Key Points

* **Paper bill governs payment amount** — always pay the amount on the paper bill, even if the online balance differs.
* **Confirmation tracking is required** — paste the confirmation code into Buildium as the check number; if none is provided, note the payment method (e.g., Zelle, PayPal).
* **Immediate credit card reimbursement** — after paying, transfer the exact bill amount from the property's bank account to the Sagareus CC via Bank of America.
* **Ledger pass-through depends on responsibility** — owner-responsible utilities (e.g., HSE Utilities) or properties with a utility recovery fee do not need charges passed through to a lease ledger.


---

## Procedure


 1. Open Buildium and navigate to **Accounting → Bills**.
 2. Select the bill to pay.
 3. Review the bill details.
 4. Navigate to the utility provider's website.
 5. Log in or pay as a guest.
 6. Cross-reference the paper bill against the online amount due.
    * Always pay the amount shown on the paper bill, even if it does not match the amount displayed online.
 7. Pay the bill in full using the Sagareus CC.
    * Copy the confirmation code / payment ID.
 8. In Buildium, select **Make Payment**.
    * Paste the confirmation number as the check number.
    * If no confirmation is provided, note the payment method (e.g., Zelle, PayPal).
 9. Navigate to Bank of America and reimburse the Sagareus CC.
    * Click **Transfer | Zelle**.
    * Select **At Bank of America** under "Between My Accounts."
    * Transfer funds from the property account (listed in Buildium as "Bank Account") to the Sagareus CC.
    * Enter the bill amount.
    * Leave frequency set to "one time; immediately."
10. In Buildium, select **Confirm Payment**.
11. In a separate Buildium window, open the correlated lease ledger.
    * Owner-responsible utilities (e.g., HSE Utilities) or properties that charge a utility recovery fee do not need funds passed through to a lease ledger.
