## Purpose

To ensure Sagareus complies with federal and Washington State requirements when processing child support income withholding orders.

## Scope

Applies to Payroll and HR staff responsible for receiving, processing, and maintaining records related to child support withholding orders.

## Procedure

### 1. Receipt of Order

* Child support income withholding orders are typically received from:
  * Washington Division of Child Support (DCS).
  * Another state's child support enforcement agency.
  * A court or attorney (rare but possible).
* Log the order in the Payroll/HR records (employee name, date received, issuing agency, deadline).

### 2. Initial Review

* Confirm the order includes:
  * Employee's name, SSN, or other identifying info.
  * Court or agency contact information.
  * Amount to be withheld and frequency.
* Verify the employee is currently employed with Sagareus.
* If employee is not employed, respond to the issuing agency immediately with the date of separation.

### 3. Notify Employee

* Provide the employee with a copy of the withholding order.
* Inform them that withholding will begin as required by law.
* Remind employee that this is a **legal requirement**, not an employer decision.

### 4. Payroll Processing


:::warning
Ensure deductions begin **no later than the first payroll period after receipt of the order**.

:::

* Payroll calculates the withholding amount as directed by the order, subject to federal and state limits:
  * Federal Consumer Credit Protection Act (CCPA) limits apply (50–65% of disposable earnings depending on circumstances).
  * If the order is from a state other than WA, verify the withholding amount is within WA state limits.
  * Rule of thumb: **Procedural rules = WA law. Order terms = Other State law.**
* Enter all information in ADP.
* ADP will forward payments to the state agency exactly as directed.

### 5. Respond to the Order

* Complete and return the **Employer Response Form** (if provided) by the deadline.
  * Confirm employee status (employed, terminated, never employed).
  * Provide wage and employment information if requested.
* Keep a copy of the response and order in the employee's confidential file.

### 6. Ongoing Administration

* Continue withholding until:
  * You receive an **official release/termination notice** from the issuing agency.
  * The employee separates from employment (notify the agency of last date worked).
* Adjust withholdings promptly if you receive a **modified order**.
* Ensure compliance with **priority rules** if multiple orders exist (child support usually takes precedence over most other garnishments).

### 7. Recordkeeping


:::warning
Maintain all child support orders, responses, and payment records for **at least 6 years** (or per company record retention policy).

:::

* Ensure all documents are stored securely in the employee's confidential payroll file.

## Responsibilities

* **HR/Payroll** — Logs and routes orders, notifies employees.
* **Payroll** — Processes deductions, ensures compliance with limits, remits payment timely.
* **HR Manager** — Reviews unusual or contested cases, oversees compliance, communicates with legal counsel if necessary.
