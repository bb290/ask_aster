# **Podcast Prompt**

Convert the attached source material into a conversational podcast episode. Follow these guidelines:

### **Tone & Voice**

Keep it light, natural, and conversational — two hosts riffing on real situations they've seen. The humor should be dry and understated, not performative. Think: two experienced property managers who've seen it all and aren't easily rattled. Clean, direct, practical. No filler. No hype.

### **Brand Feel**

This is a Sagareus episode. The brand is professional but not stiff. Confident but not arrogant. Everything you need, nothing you don't. Avoid corporate speak.

### **Structure**

Walk through each section of the brief as its own segment. Set the scene briefly, explain how a sharp property manager handles it, and drop one practical takeaway. Keep it moving — don't linger.

### **Life Lesson Closer**

End the episode with a short reflection that ties everything together under one theme. The real skill in property management (and honestly, in life) is building systems that remove ambiguity before emotions have a chance to take over. When the policy is clear, documentation is borderline excessive, everyone knows where what's happening then most conflicts can be avoided. Keep this closing grounded, not preachy. Two minute max.

### **Do Not**

* Over-explain. Trust the listener.
* Use filler affirmations ("great question!", "absolutely!")
* Make it sound like a training video
* Use the word "delve"

# Slide Deck Prompt

### **Context**

Sagareus is a property management company focused on creating long-term value through Exceptional Property Management. Our mission is to give owners peace of mind, residents a positive living experience, and our team the tools to succeed. We operate with a business-first mindset and are guided by 4 core values: **Think on your Feet, Proactive > Reactive**, **Data is King**, and **Continuous Improvement**.

### **Objective**

Create a **professional, instructional slide deck** for internal or external use that educates the audience on a specific topic, process, policy, or framework related to Sagareus operations or property management. The deck should support clarity, consistency, and alignment with Sagareus's mission and values.

### **Style**

* **Understated, clean, and functional**
* **Designed to stand alone without verbal explanation**
* Strong hierarchy with minimal on-screen text
* One core idea per slide
* No decorative visuals or filler slides

### **Tone**

Approachable and educational. Slides should guide the viewer with clarity and confidence, positioning Sagareus as a disciplined, thoughtful operator.

### **Audience**

* Internal team members (training, onboarding, retraining)
* Property owners or partners (educational presentations)
* The experience should feel consistent regardless of the audience.

## **Rules & Constraints**

### **Content Rules**

* Use clear slide titles that state the takeaway
* Break complex information into steps or sections
* Include summary or "Key Takeaways" slides where appropriate
* Reflect Sagareus values throughout:
  * Think on your Feet
  * Proactive thinking and ownership
  * Data-backed decisions and accountability
  * Continuous improvement of systems and outcomes

### **Recommended Slide Structure**

Title slide with topic and purpose

Context or "Why This Matters" slide

Section divider slides for major topics

Process, framework, or policy slides

Key takeaways / expectations slide

Closing slide reinforcing mission and accountability

### **Branding & Visual Design (mandatory)**

**Typography**

* Approved fonts only: **Inter**
* Use size and weight—not excessive color—for hierarchy
* Maintain readability for both screen and projection


**Color Palette**

* White #FFFFFF
* Light Grey #CCD1D1
* Dark Grey #616A6B
* Emerald #0B4F42
* Neutral tones dominate all slides

  \

**Output Expectations**

Produce a **slide-by-slide outline** including:

* Slide titles
* Bullet or content structure per slide
* Notes on emphasis or hierarchy
* Clear guidance that allows direct build-out in PowerPoint, Google Slides, or Keynote
