## New Hire | What to bring your first day


:::info
As part of your first-day onboarding, we will complete the Form I-9 (Employment Eligibility Verification) to confirm your authorization to work in the United States. If you have not already completed Section 1, you will be able to do so on your first day.

:::

**What to Bring:**

Please bring original, unexpired documents that establish both your identity and employment authorization. Photocopies or digital copies cannot be accepted.

You may provide either one document from List A *or* one document each from List B and List C. Examples include:

**List A** (establishes both identity and employment authorization -- only one needed):

* U.S. Passport or Passport Card
* Permanent Resident Card ("Green Card")
* Employment Authorization Document (with photo)

OR

**List B** (establishes identity):

* Driver's license or state ID card with photo
* School ID card with photo
* Voter registration card

AND

**List C** (establishes employment authorization):

* Social Security card (not labeled "Not valid for employment")
* Birth certificate (original or certified copy)
* U.S. Citizen ID card

You can view the full list of acceptable documents here: <https://www.uscis.gov/i-9-central/form-i-9-acceptable-documents>


:::warning
Please bring your selected documents on your first day, so that we can complete your verification.

Let us know if you have any questions or need clarification on which documents are acceptable.

:::

## Employer Portion | Completion and Certification of Form I-9


:::warning
**Purpose:** To ensure all employees are legally authorized to work in the United States and that the employer properly verifies and documents eligibility in compliance with federal law.

**Scope:** This SOP applies to all [HR](../../docs/role-profiles/hr-manager.md) staff and managers responsible for onboarding employees.

:::

**Step 1: Provide the I-9 Form**


1. HR provides the Form I-9 to all new hires on or before their first day of employment.
2. Employees must complete Section 1 (Employee Information and Attestation) no later than their first day of work.
3. HR should verify the employee completes Section 1 completely and accurately.
4. [I-9 Form Page 1 Section 1](https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-9.pdf)

**Step 2: Employee Presents Documentation**


1. Employee must provide original documents from the Lists of Acceptable Documents:
   * **List A:** Documents establishing identity and employment authorization (e.g., passport).
   * **List B:** Documents establishing identity only (e.g., driver's license).
   * **List C:** Documents establishing employment authorization only (e.g., Social Security card).
2. HR should review documents in person.
3. Do not accept photocopies (except in specific cases where authorized, e.g., remote verification authorized by USCIS).
4. [List of Acceptable Documents Page 2](https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-9.pdf)

**Step 3: Complete Section 2 -- Employer Review and Certification**


1. HR completes Section 2 no later than 3 business days after employee start date.
2. Steps for Section 2:
   * Record the document title, issuing authority, document number, and expiration date (if any) for the employee's documents.
   * Enter employee start date in the appropriate field.
   * View original documents employee presents.
   * Certify that the documents appear genuine and relate to the employee.
   * HR signs and dates Section 2 certifying verification.
   * Include HR staff name, title, and contact information in the certification.
3. [I-9 Form Page 1 Section 2](https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-9.pdf)

**Step 4: Retention and Storage**


1. Store the completed Form I-9 in a secure location separate from the personnel file.
2. Retention rules:
   * Keep the I-9 for 3 years after hire or 1 year after termination, whichever is later.
3. I-9s must be readily available for inspection by authorized government officials.

**Step 5: Reverification (if needed for Non-US Citizens)**


1. If the employee's employment authorization expires, HR must reverify by completing Section 3.
2. Acceptable documents for reverification: any document showing continued eligibility (e.g., green card, work permit).
3. Complete Section 3 and retain it with the original I-9.

**Compliance Tips**


:::warning
* Never discriminate based on citizenship or national origin.
* Always check original documents carefully.
* Do not specify which documents the employee must present.
* HR staff responsible for I-9s should receive training annually.

:::

**Quick Checklist for I-9 Completion**

* Provide Form I-9 to employee on/before first day.
* Employee completes Section 1.
* Employee presents original documents (List A or List B + C).
* HR reviews documents and completes Section 2.
* HR signs and dates certification.
* File securely; maintain retention rules.
* Reverify if employment authorization expires.
