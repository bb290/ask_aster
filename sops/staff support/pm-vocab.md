# Property Management Vocabulary

## Property & Unit Types

**Unit** — A single rentable space within a property (e.g., apartment, condo, ADU).

**Building** — The physical structure containing one or more rental units.

**Portfolio** — The total group of properties under Sagareus management.

**Multifamily** — A building or complex with multiple rental units.

**Single-Family Home (SFH)** — A standalone home rented as one unit.

**Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU)** — A smaller, secondary unit within or attached to a primary home.

**Detached ADU (DADU)** — A standalone secondary unit on the same lot as the main home.

**Common Area** — Shared space accessible to multiple tenants (hallways, laundry, courtyard, etc.).

**HOA (Homeowners Association)** — A governing body that sets and enforces community rules for condos/townhomes.


---

## Financial & Accounting Terms

**Rent Roll** — A report listing all units, current tenants, rent amounts, and lease dates.

**Owner Statement** — A monthly report summarizing income, expenses, and owner distributions.

**Owner Draw / Distribution** — Funds paid to the property owner after expenses are deducted.

**Management Fee** — The percentage or flat fee Sagareus earns for managing a property.

**Lease Up Fee** — The percentage or flat fee Sagareus earns for Leasing Up a property.

**Pass-Through Expense** — An expense billed back to the tenant (e.g., utilities).

**Operating Expense** — A cost required to run the property (e.g., landscaping, insurance).

**Capital Expense (CapEx)** — A large, long-term improvement (e.g., roof replacement).

**Reconciliation** — Matching financial records (e.g., bank statements vs. accounting system).


---

## Leasing & Occupancy Terms

**Market Rent** — What a unit could rent for based on current market conditions.

**Lease Term** — The duration of the rental agreement (eg 6 mo, 12 mo).

**Renewal** — Extending the lease for another term, often with a rent adjustment.

**Vacancy** — Period between tenants when a unit is not generating rent.

**Security Deposit** — Funds held as protection against tenant damage or lease default.

**Concession** — A rent incentive (e.g., "$500 off first month's rent").

**Pre-Lease** — Securing a lease before the current tenant vacates.

**Notice to Vacate (NTV)** — Tenant's formal written notice that they intend to move out.


---

## Maintenance & Operations Terms

**Maintenance Request** — A task created to track and resolve a repair or service issue.

**Preventative Maintenance** — Scheduled work done to prevent breakdowns (e.g., gutter cleaning).

**Turn Over** — The process of flipping a vacant unit for a new tenant (clean, repair, paint, inspect).

**Vendor** — A third-party service provider (e.g., plumber, landscaper).

**Bid / Estimate** — A contractor's proposed cost for a job.

**Warranty Work** — Repairs covered by a product or labor warranty.

**Scope of Work (SOW)** — The detailed description of what a contractor will do on a project.

**Emergency Maintenance** — Issues that threaten safety or property (e.g., water leak, no heat).


---

## Compliance & Legal Terms


:::warning
These terms relate to legal and regulatory requirements. Non-compliance can result in fines, lawsuits, or loss of licensure.

:::

**RCW (Revised Code of Washington)** — The collection of Washington State laws governing landlord-tenant relations.

**Just Cause Eviction Ordinance (JCEO)** — Local regulations defining lawful reasons to terminate a tenancy.

**Notice of Rent Increase (NORI)** — Legal notice sent to a tenant when rent will change.

**Fair Housing Act** — Federal law prohibiting discrimination in housing.

**Lead Disclosure** — Required notice for properties built before 1978.

**First-in-Time Rule** — Seattle-specific law requiring landlords to offer the unit to the first qualified applicant.


---

## Administrative & System Terms

**Property Management Agreement (PMA)** — Contract between Sagareus and a property owner defining scope, fees, and authority.

**Onboarding Form** — Documents collected when a new property or owner joins Sagareus.

**Asana** — Sagareus' project management platform for workflow tracking and accountability.

**Buildium** — Property management software used for leases, ledgers, and communications.

**Resident Portal** — Tenant-facing online hub for rent payments and maintenance requests.

**Owner Portal** — Owner-facing hub for reports, payments, and communications.

**Work Queue / Pipeline** — Ordered list of active tasks, often segmented by urgency or category.

## Key Policies

### Zero Discrimination Policy


:::warning
Sagareus upholds a Zero Tolerance Policy for Discrimination in all aspects of leasing, property management, and tenant relations. We strictly comply with Fair Housing laws and are committed to ensuring that no applicant, resident, or vendor is treated differently based on any class protected by federal, state, or local law. We understand that property owners may have preferences, but any request that violates Fair Housing laws—or puts Sagareus at legal or ethical risk—will not be accommodated.

:::

### Professional Photography Policy


:::info
All rental listings are marketed with professional photography to ensure high-quality presentation and brand consistency across the Sagareus portfolio. Owners are responsible for the actual cost of photography, typically ranging from $200–$300, depending on the vendor and property size. Pricing is subject to change and may vary by location or photographer. Sagareus does not profit from this policy. It is in place solely to maintain a consistent marketing standard that maximizes exposure and minimizes vacancy across all properties.

:::

### Property Tour Policy


:::info
At Sagareus, the safety and security of your property are our top priorities. To ensure a professional and controlled experience during property tours: Leasing Staff Presence: A member of the Sagareus Leasing Staff will always accompany prospective tenants during property showings. No Unattended Showings: We do not allow prospective tenants or any third parties to tour properties without a leasing staff member present. This policy helps us: Maintain the security of your property. Provide accurate and consistent information to prospective tenants. Ensure a professional and respectful showing experience.

:::

### Renter's Insurance


:::warning
Sagareus lease requires tenants to maintain renter's insurance with $100,000 liability throughout tenancy.

:::

### Property Condition Standards Policy


:::warning
Timely Maintenance: Owners must approve and fund necessary repairs promptly. Deferred maintenance or patchwork solutions that compromise safety or habitability are not permitted.

Habitability is Non-Negotiable: All properties must meet basic health, safety, and habitability standards at all times, in accordance with local and state housing laws.

Sagareus reserves the right to decline management or terminate agreements with owners who refuse to maintain their property to minimum standards.

:::

### Annual Inspection Policy


:::info
Sagareus conducts an annual Inspection of each property under management to ensure proactive maintenance, verify lease compliance, and protect the long-term value of your investment.

Inspection Scope: A visual review of interior and exterior condition with an emphasis on safety features, lease compliance and preventative maintenance.

Reporting: Owners receive a detailed report with photos, maintenance recommendations, and any tenant compliance issues.

This inspection is mandatory and cannot be waived. The $75 fee includes coordination, documentation, and reporting.

:::
