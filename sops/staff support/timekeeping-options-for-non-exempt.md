## Timekeeping Options for Non-Exempt Employees

### Option 1: ADP Mobile Timekeeping App

* Provide employees with access to ADP mobile app.
* They can clock in and out in real time when they take a call or respond to a text after hours.
* Ensures accurate timestamps and avoids relying on memory.


:::tip
**Best for:** Higher volume of after-hours calls.

:::


:::warning
**Watch out for:** Employees forgetting to log in/out → reinforce policy in training.

:::

### Option 2: Manual Adjustment by Employee

* Employees clock out at the end of their shift normally.
* If they respond to calls/texts later, they write down the time spent and add it to their daily timesheet (or submit a weekly "after-hours log").
* Supervisor reviews and adjusts the timesheet before payroll.


:::tip
**Best for:** Occasional or minimal after-hours work.

:::


:::warning
**Watch out for:** Employees underreporting time ("It was just 5 minutes") → all time counts and must be paid.

:::

### Option 3: Automatic Minimum Time Blocks

* Policy sets a minimum increment (e.g., 15 minutes) for after-hours work.
* Example: If an agent responds to a tenant call or text, they record at least 15 minutes, even if the call only took 3 minutes.
* Simplifies tracking and ensures compliance with "de minimis" rules.


:::tip
**Best for:** Quick, frequent calls/texts.

:::


:::warning
**Watch out for:** If work is longer than the increment, employees must record actual time.

:::
