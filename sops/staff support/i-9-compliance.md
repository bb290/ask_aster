## Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): I-9 Compliance

**Purpose**: To establish a standardized process for completing, storing, and maintaining Form I-9s in compliance with federal law.

### Scope

This SOP applies to all new hires, rehires, and HR personnel responsible for onboarding, personnel records, and compliance at Sagareus.

### Federal Requirements


:::warning
Form I-9 Completion Deadline: Must be completed within **3 business days** of the employee's first day of work. Retention: Keep I-9s for **3 years after hire date or 1 year after termination, whichever is later**.

:::

* Form I-9 Completion Deadline: Must be completed within 3 business days of the employee's first day of work.
* Document Verification: Employees must present original documents from List A, B, or C:
  * List A: Identity and employment authorization
  * List B: Identity only
  * List C: Employment authorization only
* Inspection: The employer must physically inspect the original documents and complete the Form I-9 accurately.
* Retention: Keep I-9s for 3 years after hire date or 1 year after termination, whichever is later.

### Procedure

#### Pre-Hire

* HR receives notification of new hire prior to first day of work.
* HR prepares the I-9 Form and informs the new employee of required documents (List A/B/C).

#### I-9 Completion

* In-Person: HR reviews and inspects original documents, completes Section 2 of Form I-9, and signs/dates the form.
* Remote / Video Completion:
  * HR may complete the I-9 via video camera if the employee is remote, following federal guidance for remote verification.
  * Employee must show original documents over video, and HR records the process in Form I-9 notes.
* Employee completes Section 1 electronically or on paper prior to first day of work.

#### Storage

Each employee has a dedicated I-9 folder under Human Resources → Personnel Files.

Store all supporting documents (copies if policy allows) in this folder.

Keep terminated employees' I-9s in a separate archived section.

#### Access and Security

Limit access to HR personnel and compliance officers.

Store physical I-9s in a locked cabinet or secure area.

Ensure electronic I-9s are password protected with audit trail enabled.

#### Audit / Inspection


:::warning
I-9s must be produced within **3 business days** of a government request.

:::

I-9s must be produced within 3 business days of a government request.

HR maintains a checklist of compliance to ensure forms are complete and documents properly reviewed.

#### Retention and Destruction

Maintain I-9s for 3 years from hire date or 1 year post-termination, whichever is later.

After the retention period, securely destroy forms (shredding for paper, secure deletion for electronic).

### Responsibilities

HR Manager: Oversee compliance, training, and audits.

HR Staff: Complete I-9s, verify documents, maintain storage, and prepare for inspections.

Owners/Managers: Notify HR of new hires prior to first day and provide support for compliance as needed.

### Notes

HR may update this SOP as federal guidance changes.

Employees and managers are required to follow this SOP to avoid penalties and maintain compliance.
