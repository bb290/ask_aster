## Responding to Unemployment Claim Notices

**Purpose:** To ensure Sagareus responds accurately and timely to all unemployment claim notices from the Washington State Employment Security Department (ESD) in order to comply with state requirements and protect the company's unemployment tax account.

### Procedure

#### 1. Notice Receipt

* ESD typically sends a **Request for Separation Information** by mail, fax, or electronically.
* HR (or designated contact) is responsible for monitoring and retrieving these notices.
* **Log the notice** in the HR/Payroll records (date received, employee name, response deadline).

#### 2. Initial Review


:::warning
Response deadline is usually **10 days**.

:::

* Verify:
  * Employee's **name** and **last date of work**.
  * Type of separation (quit, discharge, layoff, reduction in hours).
  * **Response deadline** (usually 10 days).
* Forward notice to the employee's **direct manager** if clarification is needed.

#### 3. Gather Documentation

Request from manager and/or personnel file:

* **Termination/Separation reason** (quit, layoff, discharge).
* **Supporting documents**:
  * Written warnings or performance improvement plans.
  * Final termination letter.
  * Attendance or time records (if relevant).
  * Voluntary resignation letter (if applicable).
  * Any signed acknowledgments of policies violated.
* Note: If termination was a **layoff or lack of work**, no documentation is required beyond separation details.

#### 4. Determine Response Approach

* **Approve claim (no contest):**
  * If separation was due to layoff, lack of work, reduction in hours, or no misconduct.
  * HR completes the form with accurate dates/wages and returns to ESD.
* **Contest claim (seek relief of charges):**
  * If employee quit voluntarily without good cause.
  * If employee was discharged for **misconduct** (theft, insubordination, policy violation, etc.).
  * Documentation must be attached.
* **Uncertain:**
  * Escalate to HR Director/Legal for review before responding.

#### 5. Complete the Form

* Fill in:
  * Employee's **last day worked**.
  * Reason for separation.
  * Wages and hours (if requested).
* Attach supporting documents **only if contesting**.
* Ensure dates and details **match payroll records** exactly.

#### 6. Submit Response

* Return completed form by the **deadline** (fax, mail, or electronic portal, per ESD instructions).
* Save a **copy of the submission** in the employee's personnel file and the unemployment claims log.

#### 7. Follow-Up

* Monitor for ESD determination notice.
* If ESD approves benefits despite contesting, HR may consider **appeal** if strong evidence exists.
* Track charges to Sagareus's UI account for tax rate monitoring.

#### 8. Recordkeeping


:::warning
Maintain all unemployment notices, responses, and supporting documentation for **at least 4 years** (ESD audit requirement).

:::

* Maintain all unemployment notices, responses, and supporting documentation for **at least 4 years** (ESD audit requirement).

### Responsibilities

* **HR Manager:** Oversees process, makes final decision on contesting claims, Logs notices, prepares and submits responses.
* **HR Manager/Payroll:** Confirms wages/hours reported are accurate.
* **Manager/Supervisor:** Provides documentation of performance, warnings, or resignation.
