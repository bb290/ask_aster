:::warning
Fire and gas safety is a **critical compliance area**. Washington State law mandates functional smoke detection and CO alarms. Failure to maintain these devices poses **severe liability risk** and **endangers Resident safety**. Gas leaks are habitability emergencies requiring immediate action.

:::

# Backflow Testing


:::info
Verification by a state-certified Backflow Assembly Tester (BAT) that the on-site backflow prevention assembly prevents reverse flow into the potable water system (water contamination); failures must be repaired and retested.

:::

* Applies to properties with a **fire sprinkler system** or a **pressurized irrigation system**
* Testing is done and reported annually to the local water company


---

# Smoke Alarms


:::warning
Working smoke alarms are a non-negotiable habitability requirement under Washington State Law.

* RCW 59.18.060 - Landlord responsibility
* RCW 59.18.130 - Tenant responsibility

:::

**Placement Requirements:**

* One inside every bedroom
* One outside every sleeping area (Hallway)
* One in common area of **each floor**

**Alarm Types:**

* **Battery operated**: standalone units
* **Hardwired**: Interconnected to the home's electrical system

## Sagareus Policy


:::tip
**Summary:** Install 10-year sealed battery smoke-only alarms. Never replace batteries, replace the entire unit. Always audit all alarms in a single trip.

:::

# Carbon Monoxide (CO) Detectors


:::info
Carbon Monoxide (CO) is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas produced by the incomplete combustion of fossil fuels. Washington State Law (RCW 19.27.530) mandates the installation and maintenance of CO alarms in all residential units.

:::

**Placement Requirements** (WAC 51-51-0315):

* Outside each separate sleeping area (hallways)
* On every level of the dwelling, including basements
* Inside the bedroom IF a fuel-burning appliance is located within that bedroom

# Gas Leak


:::warning
Gas Leak is a **habitability emergency**. Regardless of whether the tenant responds to troubleshooting communication, **Sagareus must take action within 24 hours** of the issue being reported. A field response team member is assigned as incident manager.

:::
