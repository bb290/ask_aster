:::warning
Heating is a core component of habitability. Without a functional heating system, a dwelling may be considered legally uninhabitable under Washington law. Pursuant to **RCW 59.18.060**, landlords are required to maintain all electrical, plumbing, heating, and other supplied systems in reasonably good working order. Washington building code (WAC 51-51-0303) requires every dwelling unit to have heating facilities capable of maintaining a minimum indoor temperature of **68F**.

:::


:::info
To ensure compliance, the heating system type for each property is recorded in the Property Maintenance project in Asana.

:::

## Loss of Heat Response


:::warning
**Loss of heat is a habitability emergency.** This means execute **Incident Response**.

:::

# Heating Types

## Electric Wall/Baseboard Heaters

* Each room has a separate heater controlled by a **separate** thermostat.


:::warning
Cadet heaters have fans, which may require cleaning. Electric heaters are a fire risk; any electrical/heating work must be completed by a **licensed electrician**, **not** a general handyman.

:::


---

## Gas / Electric Furnace

* A central furnace (often in garage, utility room or attic) serves the whole house via ductwork.
* There is one central thermostat controlling the entire home.


:::info
**Maintenance requirements:** Replace the HEPA filter once every 6 months; schedule **annual servicing** with a qualified HVAC technician.

:::


---

## Oil Furnace

* The oil is stored in an underground oil tank, usually in the backyard.
* The oil tank must be refilled periodically, the tenant is responsible for refilling.


---

## Hydronic Heating

* A boiler heats water and a pump circulates hot water via hidden piping throughout the home (radiators or radiant floor/wall panels).


:::warning
Hydronic heaters are a ticking time bomb, the pipes inside the wall can burst/leak with age. An outage for heating is also a hot water outage.

:::


---

# HVAC Servicing


:::info
**Annual HVAC servicing keeps gas/electric furnaces safe, efficient, and compliant**.

:::

Servicing is reviewed and scheduled annually during:

* Move-out
* Annual Inspection
