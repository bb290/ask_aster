:::info
Temporary relocation and rent abatement are **exception-based remedies** that apply only when a rental unit becomes **legally uninhabitable** due to conditions not caused by the resident. These determinations are made by Sagareus based on Washington law, vendor documentation, and on-site assessment. Rent abatement is not automatic and applies only for the period in which the unit is confirmed to be uninhabitable.

:::


:::warning
A **Field Service Technician assigned as Incident Manager** is responsible for evaluating habitability impact, coordinating mitigation, documenting conditions, and communicating clearly with the resident and property owner throughout the incident.

:::


---

## Habitability Determination

A unit may be deemed temporarily uninhabitable if one or more of the following conditions exist and cannot be promptly restored:

* Loss of heat (during required heating season)
* Active water intrusion or flooding
* Sewage backup
* Loss of potable water
* Electrical failure creating safety risk
* Fire damage or smoke contamination
* Structural failure or collapse risk
* Conditions requiring utilities to remain shut off for repairs


---

## Temporary Relocation Decision

### IF unit is deemed uninhabitable:

* Incident Manager escalates to Operations / Management
* Confirm:
  * Expected duration of displacement
  * Whether condition was caused by resident negligence
* Determine relocation approach:
  * Hotel (short-term, emergency)
  * Alternate housing (rare, case-specific)


---

## Rent Abatement Guidelines


:::warning
Rent abatement applies **only** when:

* The unit is confirmed uninhabitable
* The condition was not caused by resident action or neglect
* The resident is required to vacate or cannot reasonably occupy the unit

:::


---

## Re-Occupancy & Close-Out

Before resident returns:

* Incident Manager confirms:
  * Repairs fully completed
  * Utilities restored
  * Unit meets habitability standards
* Document re-occupancy date
* Finalize rent abatement calculation
* Close all incident sub-tasks


---

## Key Enforcement Notes


:::tip
* Rent abatement is **not a penalty to the owner**; it is a legal adjustment tied to habitability.
* Temporary relocation does **not** terminate the lease.
* Resident-caused damage **voids eligibility** for relocation and abatement.
* All decisions must be documented thoroughly to mitigate risk.

:::
