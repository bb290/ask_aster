:::warning
A burst pipe or uncontrolled water leak is considered a habitability and property-damage emergency. Upon report of an active leak, Sagareus is required to take immediate action, regardless of whether the resident responds to troubleshooting communication or the Owner responds to authorization requests.

:::


---

## Triage & Incident Response

### Maintenance Coordinator

* **Identify leak status and urgency**
  * Ask resident:
    * Is water actively flowing?
    * Has the main water shutoff been closed?
    * Location of leak (wall, ceiling, fixture, crawlspace, exterior)
* Review Property Maintenance Project in Asana
* Provide Emergency Plumbing Instructions to resident
* Dispatch Emergency Plumber
* Update ticket & assign Incident Manager

### Incident Manager (Field Service Technician)

* The Incident Manager is responsible for **end-to-end incident control** until stabilized.
* Minimum follow-up cadence: **Twice daily** status checks until mitigation is complete
* Immediately schedule site visit
* Critical milestones:
  * Incident call completed
  * Water source shut off
  * Vendor onsite or en route
  * Active leak fully mitigated
  * Initial damage assessment completed


---

## Communication & Repair Authorization

* Incident Manager must speak directly with the vendor to confirm:
  * Cause of failure
  * Scope of required repairs
  * Whether the issue is fully mitigated
  * Secondary damage (drywall, flooring, mold)


---

## Timeline

### T0, Vendor Onsite / Initial Triage (Same Day as Incident)

### T+1-2 Hours, Operations Manager Habitability Review

### T+3-4 Hours, Owner Notification & Authorization Request

### T+3-4 Hours, Resident Status Update

### Awaiting Owner Approval (Up to 24 Hours Unless Emergency Override Applies)

### Drying & Monitoring Phase (Typically Days 1-5)

### Secondary Repairs & Restoration Phase (2 to 21 days, depending on severity)

### Final Cleaning

### Close-Out


:::tip
**Enforcement Notes**


1. Secondary repairs are not optional once damage is confirmed
2. Drying periods cannot be rushed without risk
3. Habitability decisions are made by Sagareus, not vendors or residents
4. All steps must be documented to reduce legal and insurance exposure

:::
