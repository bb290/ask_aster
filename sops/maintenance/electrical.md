# Dangerous electrical issues


:::warning
This is an active emergency. Electrical hazards pose immediate risk to life and property.

Tickets need to be followed up every 1-2 hours, and escalated to Operations Manager.

:::


:::info
**Applies to:**

* Exposed wires, burning smell, sparks, smoke, heat, or buzzing fixture
* Water contact near outlet or switch
* Tenant reports repeated breaker trips

:::


1. Send Email - dangerous electrical safety notice & triage to the tenants
2. Dispatch to electrician, NTE $1,000 for same day service


---

# Full Loss of Power


:::warning
This is an active emergency since the home is not habitable. Tickets need to be followed up every 1-2 hours, and escalated to Operations Manager.

:::

**Immediate action:**


1. Assign a sub task to Ana (Accounting) to check if there is active outage OR unpaid bill. Hourly follow up.
2. Send self troubleshooting email/text to the tenant.

**Dispatch electrician NTE $1,000, confirm if they can do same day service.**


---

# Partial Power Outage


:::info
Kitchen, Laundry, Bathroom power outages are special cases because building codes require outlets in close proximity to water sources to be GFCI protected.

:::

**Immediate action:**


1. Send Partial outage self troubleshooting tip to the tenant.

**Dispatch electrician NTE $600, 48 hours service.**


---

# Light is out


:::info
Light fixtures are jointly maintained between housing providers and residents.

**Tenants are responsible if:**

* Bulb type is standard household, **NOT** specialty or recessed
* Fixture is **under 8ft height** and reachable with a standard step stool
* There is **NO** flickering **and NO** buzzing **and NO** burning smell

:::


---

# Specific Fixture / Circuit Issues (Ceiling Fan, Bathroom Fan/Vent, Outdoor porch light, etc)


1. Send Email - specific fixture/circuit issue triage to collect info about the incident.
2. **IF NOT** related to breaker **and NOT** related to GFCI:
   * Dispatch to electrician with NTE = $600, contractor grade material.
